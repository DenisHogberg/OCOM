# Legal Policies

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-LEGAL-POLICIES-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Policies governing the Legal Domain.

Legal Policies establish business rules for managing Contracts, Agreements, Obligations, Legal Entities, Intellectual Property, Legal Matters, Disputes, and Regulatory Objects while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, governance, and enterprise interoperability.

Policies ensure enterprise legal activities remain consistent, transparent, auditable, and compliant with organizational governance.

---

# Definition

A Legal Policy is a business rule that constrains, authorizes, or guides behavior within the Legal Domain.

Policies govern Legal Entities, Processes, Capabilities, Relationships, and Events independently of implementation technologies.

Policy semantics are defined by the Core Policy specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Legal Policies establish the rules under which legal relationships are created, reviewed, approved, executed, modified, and retired.

They provide consistent governance for contractual obligations, legal risks, intellectual property, regulatory activities, and enterprise legal accountability.

---

# Design Principles

Legal Policies shall:

- define explicit business rules;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- support auditability;
- remain version controlled;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Policy Scope

Legal Policies may govern:

- Legal Entities;
- Lifecycles;
- Relationships;
- Processes;
- Capabilities;
- Events;
- AI-assisted legal activities.

Policies may also govern interactions with other Domains.

---

# Policy Categories

Legal Policies may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Contract Policies

Policies governing contractual relationships.

Examples include:

- contract approval requirements;
- contract review requirements;
- execution authority;
- amendment governance;
- renewal governance;
- contract retention.

---

## Agreement Policies

Policies governing Agreements.

Examples include:

- negotiation authority;
- approval hierarchy;
- agreement validity;
- version governance;
- agreement retirement.

---

## Obligation Policies

Policies governing contractual commitments.

Examples include:

- obligation assignment;
- obligation ownership;
- fulfillment verification;
- breach management;
- obligation closure.

---

## Legal Entity Policies

Policies governing legal participants.

Examples include:

- legal entity registration;
- legal identity verification;
- subsidiary governance;
- corporate restructuring;
- legal entity retirement.

---

## Intellectual Property Policies

Policies governing intellectual property.

Examples include:

- ownership verification;
- trademark governance;
- patent governance;
- licensing authority;
- intellectual property transfer;
- intellectual property retirement.

---

## Legal Matter Policies

Policies governing legal activities.

Examples include:

- legal review requirements;
- legal opinion governance;
- approval requirements;
- legal documentation standards;
- legal matter closure.

---

## Dispute Policies

Policies governing legal disputes.

Examples include:

- claim registration;
- dispute escalation;
- arbitration governance;
- litigation approval;
- settlement authorization.

---

## Regulatory Policies

Policies governing legal regulatory activities.

Examples include:

- regulatory filing requirements;
- legal notice management;
- legal risk review;
- regulatory response governance;
- regulatory record retention.

---

## Governance Policies

Policies governing enterprise legal management.

Examples include:

- audit requirements;
- documentation standards;
- exception management;
- policy compliance;
- governance review.

---

# Policy Application

Policies may apply to:

- individual Legal Entities;
- Object categories;
- Processes;
- Capabilities;
- Relationships;
- Events.

Policy applicability shall be explicitly defined.

---

# Policy Enforcement

Organizations shall establish mechanisms for enforcing Legal Policies.

Enforcement mechanisms are implementation-specific and outside the scope of this specification.

Policy enforcement may result in:

- Lifecycle transitions;
- Legal Events;
- approvals;
- governance actions;
- legal reviews.

---

# Policy Governance

Legal Policies shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- approval;
- publication;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance shall ensure enterprise legal consistency.

---

# Policy Evolution

Legal Policies may evolve through:

- legal refinements;
- governance improvements;
- regulatory evolution;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Breaking Policy changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Legal Policies build upon:

- Policy
- Domain Governance
- Capability
- Lifecycle
- Object
- Contract
- Constraint

Additional Legal specifications define where individual Policies apply.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- legal workflow engines;
- contract management software;
- document repositories;
- rule engines;
- implementation technologies.

Policies remain logical business rules independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Legal implementation shall:

- define explicit Legal Policies;
- assign Policy Ownership;
- govern Policy Lifecycles;
- support Policy traceability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
