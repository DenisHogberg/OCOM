# Legal KPIs

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-LEGAL-KPIS-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) owned by the Legal Domain.

Legal KPIs measure the effectiveness, quality, responsiveness, governance, and operational performance of legal activities while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, and enterprise interoperability.

KPIs provide objective measurements for continuous improvement rather than operational control.

---

# Definition

A Legal KPI is a measurable business indicator evaluating the performance of Legal Entities, Processes, Capabilities, or governance activities.

Legal KPIs measure business outcomes rather than technical implementation.

KPI semantics are defined by the Core KPI specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Legal KPIs enable organizations to evaluate legal performance, contractual governance, legal risk management, dispute resolution, intellectual property management, and regulatory responsiveness.

They support strategic decision-making through consistent measurement.

---

# Design Principles

Legal KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- remain objective and reproducible;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- support enterprise interoperability;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

Legal KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Contract Performance

KPIs measuring contractual management.

Examples include:

- Contract Approval Time;
- Contract Execution Time;
- Contract Renewal Rate;
- Contract Amendment Frequency;
- Contract Completion Rate;
- Active Contract Count.

These KPIs evaluate contractual effectiveness.

---

## Obligation Performance

KPIs measuring contractual commitments.

Examples include:

- Obligation Fulfillment Rate;
- Overdue Obligation Count;
- Obligation Resolution Time;
- Obligation Compliance Rate;
- Outstanding Obligation Volume.

These KPIs evaluate legal commitment execution.

---

## Legal Review Performance

KPIs measuring legal review activities.

Examples include:

- Legal Review Duration;
- Review Completion Rate;
- Review Backlog;
- Approval Turnaround Time;
- Review Accuracy.

These KPIs evaluate legal responsiveness.

---

## Intellectual Property Performance

KPIs measuring intellectual property management.

Examples include:

- Active Intellectual Property Assets;
- License Renewal Rate;
- Intellectual Property Registration Time;
- License Utilization Rate;
- Intellectual Property Portfolio Growth.

These KPIs evaluate protection of enterprise assets.

---

## Dispute Performance

KPIs measuring legal dispute management.

Examples include:

- Claim Resolution Time;
- Settlement Rate;
- Litigation Duration;
- Dispute Closure Rate;
- Active Dispute Count.

These KPIs evaluate legal conflict management.

---

## Regulatory Performance

KPIs measuring regulatory legal activities.

Examples include:

- Regulatory Filing Timeliness;
- Filing Success Rate;
- Legal Risk Assessment Completion Rate;
- Regulatory Response Time;
- Outstanding Regulatory Actions.

These KPIs evaluate legal governance.

---

## Governance Performance

KPIs measuring legal governance quality.

Examples include:

- Policy Compliance Rate;
- Audit Findings;
- Documentation Completeness;
- Governance Review Completion Rate;
- Exception Resolution Time.

These KPIs evaluate legal governance maturity.

---

# KPI Ownership

The Legal Domain owns KPIs measuring:

- legal operations;
- contractual governance;
- legal performance;
- legal reviews;
- intellectual property;
- disputes;
- regulatory legal activities.

Enterprise KPI aggregation may be performed by the BI Domain.

---

# KPI Governance

Organizations shall define:

- KPI definitions;
- calculation methods;
- reporting frequency;
- ownership;
- review procedures.

Governance shall ensure KPI consistency across the enterprise.

---

# KPI Evolution

Legal KPIs may evolve through:

- additional measurements;
- refined business definitions;
- compatibility-preserving improvements.

Historical KPI interpretation shall remain stable whenever practical.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Legal KPIs build upon:

- KPI
- Capability
- Process
- Object
- Event
- Domain Governance

Additional Legal specifications identify which Entities and Capabilities contribute to individual KPIs.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- reporting platforms;
- business intelligence tools;
- analytics software;
- dashboards;
- implementation technologies.

Legal KPIs remain implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Legal implementation shall:

- define measurable Legal KPIs;
- assign KPI ownership;
- preserve KPI consistency;
- support enterprise interoperability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
