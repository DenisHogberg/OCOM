# Legal AI

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-LEGAL-AI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Artificial Intelligence (AI) participates within the Legal Domain.

AI assists legal operations by improving legal analysis, contract intelligence, obligation monitoring, legal knowledge discovery, regulatory interpretation, and decision support while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, governance, and human accountability.

AI participation remains advisory unless explicitly authorized by applicable Policies.

---

# Definition

Legal AI represents the application of AI Capabilities to Legal Entities, Processes, Relationships, Events, Policies, and enterprise legal knowledge.

AI operates within enterprise governance and does not become the owner of Legal Entities or legal decisions.

AI semantics are defined by the AI specifications of the OCOM framework.

---

# Business Meaning

AI enables organizations to better understand, manage, monitor, and evolve legal relationships through intelligent recommendations, semantic analysis, predictive insights, and operational assistance.

AI augments legal work while preserving transparency, accountability, and legal governance.

---

# Design Principles

AI participation within the Legal Domain shall:

- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- operate within applicable Policies;
- produce traceable outcomes;
- support explainability;
- remain human-governed;
- remain technology independent.

---

# AI Capability Categories

AI may support the following Legal activities.

---

## Contract Intelligence

Examples include:

- contract summarization;
- clause classification;
- contract comparison;
- amendment impact analysis;
- renewal recommendations;
- obligation extraction.

AI recommendations shall not constitute legal approval.

---

## Obligation Intelligence

Examples include:

- obligation monitoring;
- fulfillment prediction;
- deadline analysis;
- contractual dependency analysis;
- breach risk estimation.

---

## Legal Knowledge Intelligence

Examples include:

- semantic legal search;
- legal document summarization;
- precedent discovery;
- legal relationship analysis;
- enterprise legal knowledge exploration.

---

## Regulatory Intelligence

Examples include:

- regulatory change analysis;
- legal impact assessment;
- filing assistance;
- regulatory mapping;
- compliance support.

AI provides analysis rather than legal interpretation authority.

---

## Intellectual Property Intelligence

Examples include:

- intellectual property portfolio analysis;
- licensing recommendations;
- ownership relationship analysis;
- intellectual property lifecycle analysis;
- asset classification.

---

## Dispute Intelligence

Examples include:

- dispute trend analysis;
- litigation pattern identification;
- claim classification;
- settlement analysis;
- legal risk estimation.

---

## Governance Assistance

Examples include:

- Policy validation;
- contract consistency analysis;
- governance monitoring;
- audit preparation;
- legal documentation review.

---

## Knowledge Assistance

Examples include:

- enterprise legal question answering;
- semantic retrieval;
- legal terminology assistance;
- document generation;
- legal knowledge navigation.

---

# AI Decision Model

AI may:

- recommend;
- classify;
- summarize;
- analyze;
- predict;
- explain.

AI shall not inherently:

- approve Contracts;
- execute Agreements;
- issue Legal Opinions;
- authorize Litigation;
- modify Legal Entities;
- replace accountable legal decisions.

Business accountability remains outside AI.

---

# AI Inputs

AI may consume:

- Legal Entities;
- Legal Relationships;
- Legal Events;
- Legal Policies;
- Legal Lifecycles;
- legal documentation;
- enterprise business knowledge.

Input availability depends upon applicable access controls.

---

# AI Outputs

AI may produce:

- recommendations;
- analyses;
- classifications;
- summaries;
- predictions;
- legal insights;
- risk indicators;
- supporting documentation.

AI outputs do not constitute authoritative legal data unless accepted through governed business processes.

---

# Human Governance

Organizations shall define governance determining:

- when AI may advise;
- when human approval is required;
- when AI recommendations may be accepted;
- how AI analyses are reviewed;
- how accountability is maintained.

AI shall remain subject to enterprise governance.

---

# Explainability

Organizations should ensure AI-supported legal decisions remain explainable.

Explainability may include:

- reasoning summaries;
- supporting evidence;
- referenced Legal Entities;
- applied Policies;
- confidence indicators.

Implementation mechanisms remain outside the scope of this specification.

---

# AI Governance

AI participation shall comply with:

- Legal Policies;
- AI Policies;
- enterprise governance;
- applicable regulations;
- security requirements.

AI governance shall preserve trust, transparency, accountability, and legal integrity.

---

# AI Evolution

Legal AI Capabilities may evolve through:

- improved analytical models;
- expanded legal knowledge;
- enhanced recommendation quality;
- compatibility-preserving refinements.

AI evolution shall not modify Legal semantics defined by this specification.

---

Legal AI builds upon:

- AI
- Object

Additional AI specifications define enterprise-wide AI behavior.

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- foundation models;
- machine learning algorithms;
- legal software;
- contract analysis platforms;
- vector databases;
- implementation technologies.

Legal AI remains implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Legal implementation shall:

- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- ensure AI traceability;
- maintain human accountability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
