# Legal Processes

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-LEGAL-PROCESSES-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Processes performed within the Legal Domain.

Legal Processes coordinate Legal Entities to achieve legal outcomes while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, governance, and enterprise interoperability.

Processes orchestrate business activities but do not own Entities or their Lifecycles.

---

# Definition

A Legal Process is a coordinated sequence of business activities involving one or more Legal Entities.

Processes manage work performed by people, AI, or systems while Entities remain the authoritative source of legal state.

Process semantics are defined by the Core Process specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Legal Processes enable organizations to negotiate, approve, execute, monitor, and govern legal relationships consistently across the enterprise.

They coordinate legal work while preserving the independent evolution of Contracts, Obligations, Legal Entities, Intellectual Property, and Legal Matters.

---

# Design Principles

Legal Processes shall:

- coordinate Entities;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- generate Legal Events through Lifecycle transitions;
- remain auditable;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Process Categories

Legal Processes may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Contract Management

Processes governing contractual relationships.

Examples include:

- contract creation;
- contract negotiation;
- legal review;
- approval coordination;
- contract execution;
- amendment management;
- renewal management;
- contract retirement.

Contract Processes coordinate Contract Lifecycles without owning Contracts.

---

## Obligation Management

Processes governing contractual obligations.

Examples include:

- obligation definition;
- obligation assignment;
- obligation monitoring;
- obligation fulfillment;
- obligation verification;
- obligation closure.

Obligations remain independent Entities.

---

## Legal Entity Management

Processes governing legal participants.

Examples include:

- legal entity registration;
- subsidiary creation;
- corporate restructuring;
- legal entity maintenance;
- legal entity retirement.

Legal Entity identity remains preserved throughout these Processes.

---

## Intellectual Property Management

Processes governing intellectual property.

Examples include:

- intellectual property registration;
- trademark management;
- patent management;
- licensing;
- ownership transfer;
- intellectual property retirement.

Intellectual Property Processes preserve ownership independently of Products.

---

## Legal Review

Processes supporting legal evaluation.

Examples include:

- document review;
- contract review;
- regulatory review;
- legal opinion preparation;
- approval coordination.

Legal Reviews may involve multiple Entities.

---

## Dispute Management

Processes governing legal disputes.

Examples include:

- claim management;
- dispute investigation;
- negotiation;
- arbitration coordination;
- litigation support;
- settlement coordination.

Dispute Processes preserve legal history.

---

## Regulatory Governance

Processes supporting regulatory legal activities.

Examples include:

- regulatory filing;
- regulatory response;
- legal risk assessment;
- legal notice management;
- governance review.

Regulatory Processes support enterprise compliance and legal governance.

---

# Process Coordination

Legal Processes may coordinate:

- Contracts;
- Agreements;
- Obligations;
- Legal Entities;
- Intellectual Property;
- Legal Matters;
- Claims;
- Regulatory Objects.

Processes coordinate Entities without becoming their owner.

---

# Cross-Domain Coordination

Legal Processes may interact with other Domains.

Examples include:

- HR during employment agreement management;
- Finance during contractual financial obligations;
- Product during licensing;
- Affiliate during partnership agreements;
- Compliance during regulatory interpretation;
- Payments during contractual payment obligations;
- Marketing during brand usage approvals.

Each participating Domain retains ownership of its Entities.

---

# Process Governance

Legal Processes shall be governed through:

- Policies;
- Constraints;
- Lifecycles;
- Events;
- business approvals.

Governance ensures predictable enterprise legal behavior.

---

# Process Evolution

Legal Processes may evolve through:

- workflow improvements;
- organizational changes;
- governance refinements;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Process evolution shall not redefine Entity semantics.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Legal Processes build upon:

- Process
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Event
- Policy
- Capability
- Domain Governance

Additional Legal specifications define Capabilities that implement these Processes.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- workflow engines;
- BPM platforms;
- contract management software;
- document repositories;
- implementation technologies.

Legal Processes remain implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Legal implementation shall:

- define Legal Processes;
- coordinate Entities without owning them;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- generate appropriate Legal Events;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
