# Compliance Policies

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-COMPLIANCE-POLICIES-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Policies governing the Compliance Domain.

Compliance Policies establish business rules governing Compliance Objects, Processes, Capabilities, Events, and governance activities throughout the enterprise.

Policies ensure that compliance activities are performed consistently, transparently, and in accordance with enterprise obligations.

---

# Definition

A Compliance Policy is a business rule that constrains, authorizes, or guides behavior within the Compliance Domain.

Policies define expected governance behavior independently of regulatory frameworks, compliance software, or implementation technologies.

Policy semantics are defined by the Meta specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Compliance Policies establish the governance framework through which organizations satisfy internal requirements, contractual commitments, and external obligations.

Policies define acceptable business behavior rather than implementation mechanisms.

---

# Design Principles

Compliance Policies shall:

- define explicit business rules;
- support enterprise governance;
- preserve auditability;
- be consistently enforceable;
- be version controlled;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Policy Scope

Compliance Policies may apply to:

- Compliance Objects;
- Relationships;
- Lifecycles;
- Events;
- Processes;
- Capabilities;
- AI-assisted compliance activities.

Policies may also govern interactions with other Domains.

---

# Policy Categories

Compliance Policies may be organized into the following categories.

## Obligation Policies

Policies governing enterprise obligations.

Examples include:

- obligation ownership;
- obligation review frequency;
- obligation fulfillment requirements;
- obligation retention.

---

## Control Policies

Policies governing enterprise controls.

Examples include:

- control approval;
- control testing frequency;
- control effectiveness requirements;
- control retirement.

---

## Assessment Policies

Policies governing compliance assessments.

Examples include:

- assessment approval requirements;
- assessment independence;
- assessment evidence requirements;
- assessment review procedures.

---

## Audit Policies

Policies governing audit activities.

Examples include:

- audit planning;
- auditor independence;
- finding classification;
- audit reporting requirements.

---

## Evidence Policies

Policies governing evidence management.

Examples include:

- evidence collection;
- evidence verification;
- evidence retention;
- evidence access control.

---

## Remediation Policies

Policies governing corrective actions.

Examples include:

- remediation prioritization;
- remediation deadlines;
- verification requirements;
- remediation closure criteria.

---

## Regulatory Policies

Policies governing regulatory compliance.

Examples include:

- regulatory reporting;
- regulatory notification;
- policy review;
- compliance monitoring.

---

# Policy Application

Policies may apply to:

- individual Compliance Objects;
- Object categories;
- Capabilities;
- Processes;
- Events;
- Relationships.

Policy applicability shall be explicitly defined.

---

# Policy Enforcement

Organizations shall establish mechanisms for enforcing Compliance Policies.

Enforcement mechanisms are implementation-specific and outside the scope of this specification.

Policy enforcement may result in:

- Lifecycle transitions;
- Compliance Events;
- Findings;
- Remediation activities;
- escalations.

---

# Policy Governance

Compliance Policies shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- approval;
- publication;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance shall ensure enterprise-wide consistency.

---

# Policy Evolution

Compliance Policies may evolve through:

- regulatory changes;
- business requirements;
- governance improvements;
- compatibility-preserving refinements.

Breaking Policy changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Compliance Policies build upon:

- Policy
- Domain Governance
- Capability
- Lifecycle
- Object
- Contract
- Constraint

Additional Compliance specifications define where individual Policies apply.

---

# Independence

The Compliance Policies specification does not prescribe:

- regulatory frameworks;
- GRC platforms;
- workflow engines;
- implementation technologies.

Policies remain logical business rules independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Compliance implementation shall:

- define explicit Compliance Policies;
- assign Policy Ownership;
- govern Policy lifecycles;
- support Policy traceability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
