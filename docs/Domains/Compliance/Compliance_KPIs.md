# Compliance Key Performance Indicators

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-COMPLIANCE-KPI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) applicable to the Compliance Domain.

Compliance KPIs provide standardized measures for evaluating the effectiveness, quality, responsiveness, and maturity of enterprise compliance activities.

KPIs support governance, continuous improvement, regulatory transparency, and enterprise accountability.

---

# Definition

A Compliance KPI is a measurable business indicator used to evaluate the performance of the Compliance Domain.

KPIs measure governance outcomes rather than implementation technologies, compliance software, or regulatory platforms.

---

# Business Meaning

Compliance KPIs provide objective insight into how effectively the enterprise manages obligations, controls, assessments, audits, evidence, and remediation activities.

They support informed governance decisions and continuous improvement of enterprise compliance.

---

# Design Principles

Compliance KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- align with Compliance Capabilities;
- remain objectively measurable;
- preserve semantic consistency;
- support historical comparison;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

Compliance KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

## Obligation Performance

Measures evaluating compliance obligations.

Examples include:

- obligation fulfillment rate;
- overdue obligations;
- obligation review timeliness;
- obligation coverage.

---

## Control Performance

Measures evaluating enterprise controls.

Examples include:

- control effectiveness;
- control coverage;
- control failure rate;
- control review completion rate.

---

## Assessment Performance

Measures evaluating compliance assessments.

Examples include:

- assessment completion rate;
- assessment approval time;
- assessment backlog;
- assessment accuracy.

---

## Audit Performance

Measures evaluating audit activities.

Examples include:

- audit completion rate;
- audit timeliness;
- findings per audit;
- audit closure time.

---

## Evidence Quality

Measures evaluating governance evidence.

Examples include:

- evidence completeness;
- evidence verification rate;
- evidence availability;
- evidence retention compliance.

---

## Remediation Performance

Measures evaluating corrective actions.

Examples include:

- remediation completion rate;
- remediation cycle time;
- overdue remediation actions;
- remediation verification success rate.

---

## Governance Effectiveness

Measures evaluating overall compliance performance.

Examples include:

- policy compliance rate;
- regulatory compliance rate;
- recurring findings;
- compliance maturity.

---

# KPI Ownership

Every KPI shall have an owning Domain.

The Compliance Domain owns KPIs evaluating Compliance Capabilities and Compliance Objects.

Ownership of a KPI does not imply ownership of referenced operational Objects.

---

# KPI Calculation

Organizations shall define calculation methods appropriate to their governance environment.

Calculation methods may include:

- percentages;
- ratios;
- trend analysis;
- duration measurements;
- aggregated indicators.

Calculation formulas are implementation-specific.

---

# KPI Interpretation

KPIs shall be interpreted within their governance context.

Interpretation may consider:

- regulatory obligations;
- enterprise risk;
- historical performance;
- governance objectives;
- applicable Policies.

Thresholds and performance targets are organization-specific.

---

# KPI Governance

Compliance KPIs shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- business definitions;
- calculation rules;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance ensures enterprise-wide consistency.

---

# KPI Evolution

Compliance KPIs may evolve through:

- additional indicators;
- refined business definitions;
- improved calculation methods;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Breaking semantic changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Compliance KPIs build upon:

- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Domain
- Policy
- Governance

Additional Compliance specifications define the business context in which KPIs are evaluated.

---

# Independence

The Compliance KPI specification does not prescribe:

- governance dashboards;
- compliance software;
- reporting platforms;
- monitoring systems;
- implementation technologies.

KPIs remain logical business measures independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Compliance implementation shall:

- define measurable Compliance KPIs;
- assign KPI Ownership;
- provide consistent KPI definitions;
- govern KPI evolution;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
