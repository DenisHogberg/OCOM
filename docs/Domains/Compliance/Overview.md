# Compliance Domain

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-COMPLIANCE-README-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This specification defines the Compliance Domain within the OCOM Specification.

The Compliance Domain manages business Objects representing compliance obligations, assessments, controls, findings, and regulatory evidence.

The Domain provides capabilities for compliance management, policy enforcement, control evaluation, regulatory monitoring, audit support, and governance while preserving Object Ownership and interoperability with other enterprise Domains.

---

# Scope

The Compliance Domain defines:

- managed Compliance Objects;
- compliance Relationships;
- Compliance Events;
- compliance Lifecycles;
- compliance Processes;
- Compliance Capabilities;
- Compliance KPIs;
- Compliance Policies;
- AI capabilities within the Compliance Domain.

The Domain does not define legal frameworks, regulations, governance software, audit platforms, or implementation technologies.

---

# Objectives

The objectives of the Compliance Domain are to:

- evaluate business activities against governing requirements;
- manage compliance obligations;
- monitor enterprise controls;
- support regulatory and internal audits;
- provide standardized compliance Capabilities;
- ensure enterprise-wide governance consistency.

---

# Domain Responsibilities

The Compliance Domain is responsible for:

- compliance obligation management;
- control management;
- compliance assessment;
- policy compliance evaluation;
- regulatory monitoring;
- audit support;
- compliance reporting;
- remediation tracking.

Responsibilities outside this scope belong to their respective Domains.

# Integration

The Compliance Domain collaborates with other Domains through:

- References;
- Relationships;
- Events;
- Contracts;
- Policies.

The Compliance Domain evaluates business activities performed by other Domains but does not assume ownership of their operational Objects.

# Independence

The Compliance Domain does not prescribe:

- regulatory frameworks;
- governance software;
- compliance platforms;
- audit systems;
- implementation technologies.

Organizations remain free to implement Compliance capabilities using technologies appropriate to their operational environment.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Compliance implementation shall:

- define managed Compliance Objects;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- support standardized Domain integration;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
