# Operations KPIs

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-OPERATIONS-KPIS-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) used by the Operations Domain.

Operational KPIs measure execution effectiveness, operational efficiency, throughput, quality, reliability, and continuous improvement while preserving Object Ownership and enterprise interoperability.

KPIs evaluate business performance rather than implementation-specific system metrics.

---

# Definition

An Operational KPI is a measurable business indicator that evaluates the performance of Operational Entities, Operational Processes, and Operational Capabilities.

Operational KPIs support governance, planning, optimization, and decision-making.

Metric definitions are governed by the Core KPI specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Operational KPIs provide objective visibility into enterprise execution.

They enable organizations to evaluate how efficiently work is planned, assigned, executed, coordinated, and completed while maintaining quality and governance.

---

# Design Principles

Operational KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- remain objectively measurable;
- support enterprise governance;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- remain comparable over time;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

Operational KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Execution KPIs

Execution KPIs evaluate operational delivery.

Examples include:

- Work Items Created;
- Work Items Completed;
- Work Completion Rate;
- Task Completion Rate;
- Operational Throughput;
- Execution Success Rate.

Execution KPIs measure operational productivity.

---

## Time KPIs

Time KPIs evaluate operational responsiveness.

Examples include:

- Average Planning Time;
- Average Assignment Time;
- Average Execution Time;
- Average Completion Time;
- Cycle Time;
- Lead Time.

Time KPIs measure execution efficiency.

---

## Quality KPIs

Quality KPIs evaluate operational reliability.

Examples include:

- Rework Rate;
- Task Reopen Rate;
- Operational Error Rate;
- Procedure Compliance Rate;
- Execution Accuracy;
- Quality Score.

Quality KPIs measure consistency and operational excellence.

---

## Capacity KPIs

Capacity KPIs evaluate operational resource utilization.

Examples include:

- Resource Utilization;
- Capacity Utilization;
- Queue Utilization;
- Assignment Load;
- Workload Distribution;
- Capacity Availability.

Capacity KPIs measure operational balance.

---

## Queue KPIs

Queue KPIs evaluate execution flow.

Examples include:

- Queue Length;
- Queue Waiting Time;
- Queue Processing Rate;
- Queue Aging;
- Queue Backlog;
- Queue Resolution Rate.

Queue KPIs measure operational flow efficiency.

---

## Planning KPIs

Planning KPIs evaluate operational planning.

Examples include:

- Plan Completion Rate;
- Milestone Achievement Rate;
- Schedule Adherence;
- Planning Accuracy;
- Forecast Accuracy.

Planning KPIs evaluate execution predictability.

---

## Governance KPIs

Governance KPIs evaluate operational compliance.

Examples include:

- Policy Compliance Rate;
- Procedure Adoption Rate;
- Audit Findings;
- Operational Exceptions;
- Governance Violations;
- Approval Completion Rate.

Governance KPIs measure operational control.

---

## Improvement KPIs

Improvement KPIs evaluate organizational learning.

Examples include:

- Continuous Improvement Initiatives;
- Automation Adoption Rate;
- Process Improvement Rate;
- Operational Efficiency Gain;
- Standardization Rate.

Improvement KPIs measure organizational maturity.

---

# KPI Ownership

The Operations Domain owns KPIs that evaluate Operational Entities, Operational Processes, and Operational Capabilities.

Other Domains may consume Operational KPIs without assuming ownership.

---

# KPI Governance

Operational KPIs shall be governed through:

- standardized definitions;
- approved calculation methods;
- measurement consistency;
- auditability;
- enterprise governance.

Organizations shall ensure KPI definitions remain stable over time.

---

# KPI Evolution

Operational KPIs may evolve through:

- additional business metrics;
- refined measurement methods;
- improved operational visibility;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Historical KPI meaning shall remain preserved.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Operational KPIs build upon:

- KPI
- Capability
- Process
- Lifecycle
- Event
- Domain Governance

Operational KPIs evaluate enterprise execution independently of implementation technologies.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- reporting platforms;
- BI tools;
- ERP systems;
- workflow engines;
- implementation technologies.

Operational KPIs remain implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Operations implementation shall:

- define standardized Operational KPIs;
- preserve KPI consistency;
- support objective measurement;
- enable enterprise interoperability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
