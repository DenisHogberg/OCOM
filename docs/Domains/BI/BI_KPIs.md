# Business Intelligence Key Performance Indicators

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-BI-KPI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) applicable to the Business Intelligence (BI) Domain.

BI KPIs provide standardized measures for evaluating the effectiveness, quality, availability, and business outcomes of BI Capabilities and managed Analytical Objects.

KPIs support governance, analytical transparency, continuous improvement, and enterprise decision-making.

---

# Definition

A BI KPI is a measurable business indicator used to evaluate the performance of the Business Intelligence Domain.

KPIs measure analytical business outcomes rather than reporting platforms, data warehouses, or implementation technologies.

---

# Business Meaning

BI KPIs provide objective insight into how effectively analytical capabilities transform enterprise business facts into trusted business knowledge.

They support governance, analytical quality, operational visibility, and strategic decision-making.

---

# Design Principles

BI KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- align with Domain Capabilities;
- remain objectively measurable;
- preserve semantic consistency;
- support historical comparison;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

BI KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

## Metric Performance

Measures evaluating enterprise metrics.

Examples include:

- KPI publication rate;
- metric validation rate;
- metric freshness;
- metric consistency.

---

## Reporting Performance

Measures evaluating reporting activities.

Examples include:

- report publication timeliness;
- report availability;
- report completeness;
- report accuracy.

---

## Dashboard Performance

Measures evaluating dashboards.

Examples include:

- dashboard availability;
- dashboard refresh timeliness;
- dashboard consistency;
- dashboard usage.

---

## Forecast Performance

Measures evaluating predictive analytics.

Examples include:

- forecast accuracy;
- forecast timeliness;
- forecast coverage;
- forecast validation rate.

---

## Insight Quality

Measures evaluating analytical outcomes.

Examples include:

- anomaly detection precision;
- recommendation acceptance rate;
- insight relevance;
- analytical consistency.

---

## Operational Efficiency

Measures evaluating analytical operations.

Examples include:

- analytical processing time;
- analytical throughput;
- processing backlog;
- analytical exception rate.

---

# KPI Ownership

Every KPI shall have an owning Domain.

The BI Domain owns KPIs evaluating BI Capabilities and Analytical Objects.

Ownership of a KPI does not imply ownership of referenced operational Objects.

---

# KPI Calculation

Organizations shall define calculation methods appropriate to their analytical environment.

Calculation methods may include:

- aggregation;
- ratios;
- percentages;
- trend analysis;
- time-based measurements.

Calculation formulas are implementation-specific.

---

# KPI Interpretation

KPIs shall be interpreted within their business context.

Interpretation may consider:

- historical performance;
- business objectives;
- analytical maturity;
- operational priorities;
- applicable Policies.

Thresholds and performance targets are organization-specific.

---

# KPI Governance

BI KPIs shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- business definitions;
- calculation rules;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance ensures enterprise-wide analytical consistency.

---

# KPI Evolution

BI KPIs may evolve through:

- additional indicators;
- refined business definitions;
- improved calculation methods;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Breaking semantic changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

BI KPIs build upon:

- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Domain
- Policy
- Governance

Additional BI specifications define the business context in which KPIs are evaluated.

---

# Independence

The BI KPI specification does not prescribe:

- dashboard software;
- reporting platforms;
- analytics engines;
- monitoring systems;
- implementation technologies.

KPIs remain logical business measures independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming BI implementation shall:

- define measurable BI KPIs;
- assign KPI Ownership;
- provide consistent KPI definitions;
- govern KPI evolution;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
