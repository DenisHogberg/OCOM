# Business Intelligence Artificial Intelligence

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-BI-AI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) within the Business Intelligence (BI) Domain.

AI capabilities assist analytical operations by interpreting business context, generating analytical insights, supporting enterprise decision-making, and automating authorized analytical activities while preserving Object Ownership, analytical governance, and enterprise consistency.

---

# Definition

BI AI represents AI capabilities operating within the Business Intelligence Domain.

AI assists analytical activities but does not alter Domain responsibilities, Object Ownership, or governance.

AI behavior shall remain consistent with the AI specifications defined by the OCOM Specification.

---

# Business Meaning

AI enhances enterprise analytics through intelligent interpretation, forecasting, anomaly detection, pattern recognition, and decision support.

AI functions as an analytical capability rather than an autonomous decision-making authority.

Business accountability remains with the owning organization.

---

# Design Principles

BI AI shall:

- operate on Analytical Objects;
- preserve Object Identity;
- respect Domain Ownership;
- comply with applicable Policies;
- produce explainable outputs;
- remain technology independent.

---

# AI Scope

BI AI may assist with:

- analytical interpretation;
- trend identification;
- anomaly detection;
- forecasting;
- scenario analysis;
- recommendation generation;
- business summarization;
- decision support.

Organizations may define additional AI capabilities.

---

# AI Context

BI AI may consume business context including:

- Analytical Objects;
- Relationships;
- Events;
- Lifecycles;
- Policies;
- Contracts;
- enterprise Memory;
- historical business activity.

Context availability is organization-specific.

---

# AI Outputs

BI AI may produce:

- recommendations;
- classifications;
- predictions;
- summaries;
- explanations;
- suggested actions;
- confidence assessments.

AI outputs shall not become business facts until accepted through applicable governance.

---

# AI Decision Support

BI AI may assist business decisions by:

- identifying business trends;
- highlighting analytical anomalies;
- recommending strategic actions;
- prioritizing analytical investigations;
- forecasting business outcomes.

Final business authority remains outside AI unless organizational Policies explicitly authorize automated execution.

---

# AI Governance

BI AI shall operate under:

- Domain Governance;
- AI Governance;
- applicable Policies;
- applicable Contracts;
- applicable Constraints.

AI activities shall remain traceable and auditable.

---

# AI Memory

BI AI may consume and contribute to enterprise Memory.

Memory usage shall comply with:

- Memory Governance;
- evidence requirements;
- retention policies;
- privacy requirements.

Memory Ownership remains unchanged.

---

# AI Collaboration

BI AI may collaborate with AI capabilities operating in other Domains.

Collaboration shall occur through:

- Events;
- Capabilities;
- Contracts;
- governed business context.

Direct access to another Domain's internal implementation shall not be assumed.

---

# AI Evolution

BI AI capabilities may evolve through:

- improved reasoning;
- enhanced analytical capabilities;
- expanded business context;
- updated Policies;
- compatibility-preserving improvements.

Breaking behavioral changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

BI AI builds upon:

- AI
- AI Governance
- Memory
- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Policy
- Contract
- Domain Governance

Additional BI specifications define the business context in which AI operates.

---

# Independence

The BI AI specification does not prescribe:

- AI models;
- machine learning algorithms;
- large language models;
- analytics platforms;
- implementation technologies.

Organizations remain free to implement AI capabilities using technologies appropriate to their operational environment.

---

# Conformance

A conforming BI implementation shall:

- define AI capabilities within the BI Domain;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- comply with Domain Governance;
- support explainable and traceable AI behavior;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
