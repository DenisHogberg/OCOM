# Domain Governance

**Document ID:** DOM-GOVERNANCE-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the governance principles for Domains within the OCOM Specification.

Domain Governance establishes the responsibilities, decision-making processes, and management practices required to ensure that Domains remain consistent, interoperable, and aligned with enterprise objectives throughout their lifecycle.

---

# Definition

Domain Governance is the framework through which a Domain is managed, controlled, and evolved.

Governance ensures that Domain responsibilities, ownership, policies, and operational behavior remain consistent over time.

---

# Business Meaning

Effective Domain Governance enables organizations to evolve enterprise architecture in a controlled manner while maintaining operational integrity, accountability, and interoperability.

Governance supports sustainable growth without sacrificing architectural consistency.

---

# Design Principles

Domain Governance shall:

- establish clear accountability;
- preserve Domain ownership;
- support controlled evolution;
- ensure policy compliance;
- maintain interoperability;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Governance Scope

Governance applies to:

- Domain definition;
- Domain ownership;
- Domain capabilities;
- managed Objects;
- Policies;
- Constraints;
- integration;
- communication;
- Events;
- lifecycle evolution.

---

# Domain Ownership

Every Domain shall have explicitly defined ownership.

Ownership is responsible for:

- business direction;
- governance decisions;
- policy approval;
- capability management;
- architectural consistency.

Ownership shall remain identifiable throughout the Domain lifecycle.

---

# Governance Responsibilities

Domain Governance shall define responsibility for:

- change approval;
- policy management;
- capability evolution;
- Object ownership;
- integration management;
- conflict resolution.

Responsibilities shall be documented.

---

# Decision Authority

Governance shall define how architectural decisions are made.

Decision authority may include:

- approval of structural changes;
- introduction of new capabilities;
- retirement of obsolete capabilities;
- policy modifications;
- boundary changes.

Decision processes are organization-specific.

---

# Policy Governance

Every Domain shall govern the Policies applicable to its operational responsibilities.

Policy governance includes:

- creation;
- review;
- approval;
- publication;
- retirement.

Policy enforcement mechanisms are outside the scope of this specification.

---

# Change Management

Governance shall establish controlled processes for Domain change.

Changes may include:

- structural modifications;
- capability additions;
- policy updates;
- integration changes;
- ownership changes.

Changes should preserve compatibility whenever practical.

---

# Version Management

Every Domain should maintain version information.

Version management should support:

- traceability;
- compatibility;
- migration;
- historical reference.

Version numbering is implementation-specific.

---

# Compliance

Governance shall ensure that the Domain remains compliant with:

- OCOM Specifications;
- organizational Policies;
- applicable Constraints;
- approved Contracts.

Compliance assessment is organization-specific.

---

# Conflict Resolution

Organizations shall define procedures for resolving conflicts involving:

- ownership;
- responsibilities;
- Policies;
- integration;
- capability overlap.

Conflict resolution shall preserve enterprise consistency.

---

# Auditability

Governance activities should be auditable.

Audit information may include:

- governance decisions;
- approvals;
- ownership changes;
- policy changes;
- version history.

Audit implementation is organization-specific.

---

# Evolution

Governance shall support continuous Domain evolution while preserving:

- Object Identity;
- ownership;
- interoperability;
- compatibility;
- operational integrity.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Domain Governance builds upon:

- Domain
- Domain Principles
- Domain Architecture
- Domain Boundary
- Domain Integration
- Domain Communication
- Domain Events
- Policy
- Ownership
- Contract

Governance applies these concepts to the management of Domains.

---

# Independence

The Domain Governance specification does not prescribe:

- organizational structures;
- governance committees;
- approval workflows;
- software platforms;
- implementation technologies.

Organizations remain free to establish governance processes appropriate to their operational environments.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Domain shall:

- define explicit governance responsibilities;
- establish identifiable ownership;
- support controlled change management;
- preserve interoperability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
