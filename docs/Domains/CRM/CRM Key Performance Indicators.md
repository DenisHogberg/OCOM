# CRM Key Performance Indicators

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-CRM-KPI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) applicable to the CRM Domain.

KPIs provide standardized measures for evaluating the effectiveness, quality, and business outcomes of CRM Capabilities and managed Objects.

KPIs support objective assessment without prescribing organizational targets or implementation methods.

---

# Definition

A CRM KPI is a measurable business indicator used to evaluate the performance of the CRM Domain.

KPIs measure business outcomes rather than implementation details or technical performance.

---

# Business Meaning

CRM KPIs enable organizations to evaluate how effectively customer relationships are established, maintained, and improved.

KPIs support business decision-making, governance, and continuous improvement.

---

# Design Principles

CRM KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- align with Domain Capabilities;
- remain objectively measurable;
- preserve semantic consistency;
- support historical comparison;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

CRM KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

## Customer Growth

Measures related to customer acquisition and expansion.

Examples include:

- customer acquisition rate;
- customer activation rate;
- customer growth rate;
- profile completion rate.

---

## Customer Engagement

Measures related to customer participation and activity.

Examples include:

- engagement rate;
- campaign participation rate;
- interaction frequency;
- communication response rate.

---

## Customer Retention

Measures related to maintaining customer relationships.

Examples include:

- customer retention rate;
- reactivation rate;
- churn rate;
- loyalty participation rate.

---

## Communication Effectiveness

Measures evaluating customer communications.

Examples include:

- communication delivery rate;
- communication open rate;
- communication response rate;
- communication completion rate.

---

## Data Quality

Measures evaluating CRM Object quality.

Examples include:

- profile completeness;
- relationship completeness;
- duplicate rate;
- data consistency rate.

---

# KPI Ownership

Every KPI shall have an owning Domain.

The CRM Domain owns KPIs that evaluate CRM Capabilities and CRM-managed Objects.

Ownership of a KPI does not imply ownership of referenced Objects.

---

# KPI Calculation

Organizations shall define calculation methods appropriate to their operational environment.

Calculation methods may include:

- aggregation;
- ratios;
- percentages;
- trend analysis;
- time-based measurements.

Calculation formulas are implementation-specific.

---

# KPI Interpretation

KPIs shall be interpreted within their business context.

Interpretation may consider:

- historical performance;
- organizational objectives;
- business strategy;
- applicable Policies;
- operational constraints.

Thresholds and targets are organization-specific.

---

# KPI Governance

CRM KPIs shall be governed through:

- ownership;
- business definitions;
- calculation rules;
- version management;
- review processes.

Governance ensures consistent interpretation across the enterprise.

---

# KPI Evolution

CRM KPIs may evolve through:

- additional indicators;
- refined definitions;
- updated calculation methods;
- compatibility-preserving improvements.

Breaking semantic changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

CRM KPIs build upon:

- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Domain
- Policy
- Governance

Additional CRM specifications define the business context in which KPIs are evaluated.

---

# Independence

The CRM KPI specification does not prescribe:

- reporting platforms;
- dashboards;
- business intelligence tools;
- analytical technologies.

KPIs remain logical business measures independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming CRM implementation shall:

- define measurable business KPIs;
- assign KPI ownership;
- provide consistent KPI definitions;
- govern KPI evolution;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
