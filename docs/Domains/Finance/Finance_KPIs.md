# Financial Key Performance Indicators

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-FINANCE-KPI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) applicable to the Finance Domain.

Financial KPIs provide standardized measures for evaluating the effectiveness, quality, accuracy, and business outcomes of Financial Capabilities and managed Financial Objects.

KPIs support governance, operational transparency, financial integrity, and continuous improvement.

---

# Definition

A Financial KPI is a measurable business indicator used to evaluate the performance of the Finance Domain.

KPIs measure financial business outcomes rather than technical implementation or organizational structure.

---

# Business Meaning

Financial KPIs provide objective insight into the financial health and operational effectiveness of enterprise financial activities.

They support informed decision-making, governance, auditability, and continuous improvement.

---

# Design Principles

Financial KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- align with Domain Capabilities;
- remain objectively measurable;
- preserve semantic consistency;
- support historical comparison;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

Financial KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

## Accounting Performance

Measures evaluating accounting activities.

Examples include:

- journal posting accuracy;
- journal processing rate;
- accounting exception rate;
- adjustment frequency.

---

## Reconciliation Performance

Measures evaluating reconciliation activities.

Examples include:

- reconciliation completion rate;
- reconciliation accuracy;
- unresolved reconciliation rate;
- reconciliation cycle time.

---

## Financial Close Performance

Measures evaluating financial closing activities.

Examples include:

- financial close duration;
- close completion rate;
- reporting readiness;
- close exception rate.

---

## Financial Quality

Measures evaluating financial data quality.

Examples include:

- financial correction rate;
- duplicate posting rate;
- ledger consistency;
- balance accuracy.

---

## Operational Efficiency

Measures evaluating financial operations.

Examples include:

- average processing time;
- financial throughput;
- processing backlog;
- exception resolution time.

---

## Financial Reporting

Measures evaluating reporting capabilities.

Examples include:

- report publication timeliness;
- reporting completeness;
- reporting accuracy;
- reporting consistency.

---

# KPI Ownership

Every KPI shall have an owning Domain.

The Finance Domain owns KPIs evaluating Financial Capabilities and Financial Objects.

Ownership of a KPI does not imply ownership of referenced Objects.

---

# KPI Calculation

Organizations shall define calculation methods appropriate to their operational environment.

Calculation methods may include:

- aggregation;
- ratios;
- percentages;
- trend analysis;
- time-based measurements.

Calculation formulas are implementation-specific.

---

# KPI Interpretation

KPIs shall be interpreted within their business context.

Interpretation may consider:

- historical performance;
- business objectives;
- financial governance;
- operational priorities;
- applicable Policies.

Thresholds and performance targets are organization-specific.

---

# KPI Governance

Financial KPIs shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- business definitions;
- calculation rules;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance ensures enterprise-wide consistency.

---

# KPI Evolution

Financial KPIs may evolve through:

- additional indicators;
- refined business definitions;
- improved calculation methods;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Breaking semantic changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Financial KPIs build upon:

- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Domain
- Policy
- Governance

Additional Finance specifications define the business context in which KPIs are evaluated.

---

# Independence

The Financial KPI specification does not prescribe:

- ERP dashboards;
- accounting software;
- reporting platforms;
- analytics tools;
- implementation technologies.

KPIs remain logical business measures independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Finance implementation shall:

- define measurable Financial KPIs;
- assign KPI Ownership;
- provide consistent KPI definitions;
- govern KPI evolution;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
