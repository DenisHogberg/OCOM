# Product Policies

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-PRODUCT-POLICIES-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Policies governing the Product Domain.

Product Policies establish business rules governing Product Objects, Processes, Capabilities, Events, and commercial governance throughout the enterprise.

Policies ensure Product Objects are managed consistently, transparently, and in accordance with enterprise objectives.

---

# Definition

A Product Policy is a business rule that constrains, authorizes, or guides behavior within the Product Domain.

Policies define expected commercial behavior independently of product catalog systems, commerce platforms, or implementation technologies.

Policy semantics are defined by the Meta specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Product Policies establish the governance framework for enterprise commercial offerings.

They ensure Products, Services, Offerings, Bundles, and Subscription Plans evolve consistently throughout their Lifecycles while preserving enterprise interoperability.

---

# Design Principles

Product Policies shall:

- define explicit commercial business rules;
- support Product governance;
- preserve commercial consistency;
- remain auditable;
- be version controlled;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Policy Scope

Product Policies may apply to:

- Product Objects;
- Relationships;
- Lifecycles;
- Events;
- Processes;
- Capabilities;
- AI-assisted Product activities.

Policies may also govern interactions with other Domains.

---

# Policy Categories

Product Policies may be organized into the following categories.

## Product Governance Policies

Policies governing Product management.

Examples include:

- Product approval;
- Product ownership;
- Product versioning;
- Product retirement.

---

## Offering Policies

Policies governing commercial Offerings.

Examples include:

- Offering publication;
- Offering withdrawal;
- market eligibility;
- commercial approval.

---

## Bundle Policies

Policies governing Bundles.

Examples include:

- bundle composition;
- component compatibility;
- bundle approval;
- bundle retirement.

---

## Subscription Policies

Policies governing Subscription Plans.

Examples include:

- subscription eligibility;
- renewal rules;
- plan availability;
- retirement criteria.

---

## Configuration Policies

Policies governing configurable Products.

Examples include:

- feature compatibility;
- configuration validation;
- option dependencies;
- template approval.

---

## Portfolio Governance Policies

Policies governing Product portfolios.

Examples include:

- portfolio review;
- portfolio categorization;
- portfolio rationalization;
- lifecycle governance.

---

## Commercial Compliance Policies

Policies governing commercial consistency.

Examples include:

- naming conventions;
- classification standards;
- publication requirements;
- documentation requirements.

---

# Policy Application

Policies may apply to:

- individual Product Objects;
- Object categories;
- Capabilities;
- Processes;
- Events;
- Relationships.

Policy applicability shall be explicitly defined.

---

# Policy Enforcement

Organizations shall establish mechanisms for enforcing Product Policies.

Enforcement mechanisms are implementation-specific and outside the scope of this specification.

Policy enforcement may result in:

- Lifecycle transitions;
- Product Events;
- commercial approvals;
- governance reviews;
- portfolio updates.

---

# Policy Governance

Product Policies shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- approval;
- publication;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance shall ensure enterprise-wide commercial consistency.

---

# Policy Evolution

Product Policies may evolve through:

- business strategy changes;
- market evolution;
- governance improvements;
- compatibility-preserving refinements.

Breaking Policy changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Product Policies build upon:

- Policy
- Domain Governance
- Capability
- Lifecycle
- Object
- Contract
- Constraint

Additional Product specifications define where individual Policies apply.

---

# Independence

The Product Policies specification does not prescribe:

- product catalog software;
- commerce platforms;
- workflow engines;
- implementation technologies.

Policies remain logical business rules independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Product implementation shall:

- define explicit Product Policies;
- assign Policy Ownership;
- govern Policy lifecycles;
- support Policy traceability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
