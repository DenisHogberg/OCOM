# Product AI

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-PRODUCT-AI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Artificial Intelligence (AI) participates within the Product Domain.

AI assists Product management by supporting commercial analysis, recommendation generation, information synthesis, and decision preparation while preserving human governance, Object Ownership, and Lifecycle integrity.

AI participation remains advisory unless explicitly authorized by applicable Policies.

---

# Definition

Product AI represents the application of AI Capabilities to Product Objects and Product Processes.

AI operates on Product knowledge, Relationships, Events, Policies, and Lifecycles without assuming Ownership of Product Objects or governance responsibilities.

AI semantics are defined by the AI specifications of the OCOM framework.

---

# Business Meaning

AI enables organizations to improve the quality, consistency, and efficiency of Product management while maintaining enterprise governance.

AI augments commercial decision-making but does not replace accountable business ownership.

---

# Design Principles

AI participation within the Product Domain shall:

- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- operate within applicable Policies;
- produce traceable outcomes;
- support explainability;
- remain human-governed;
- remain technology independent.

---

# AI Capability Categories

AI may support the following Product activities.

## Product Analysis

Examples include:

- Product portfolio analysis;
- Product classification;
- Product comparison;
- Product quality assessment;
- Product consistency analysis.

---

## Offering Optimization

Examples include:

- offering recommendations;
- pricing scenario preparation;
- commercial positioning;
- market segmentation support;
- portfolio balancing.

---

## Configuration Assistance

Examples include:

- configuration validation;
- feature compatibility analysis;
- configuration recommendations;
- option dependency analysis.

---

## Lifecycle Assistance

Examples include:

- lifecycle readiness assessment;
- publication recommendations;
- retirement recommendations;
- lifecycle anomaly detection.

---

## Governance Assistance

Examples include:

- policy validation;
- documentation review;
- governance consistency analysis;
- approval preparation.

---

## Knowledge Assistance

Examples include:

- Product summarization;
- specification generation;
- relationship discovery;
- semantic search;
- documentation assistance.

---

# AI Decision Model

AI may:

- recommend;
- classify;
- summarize;
- analyze;
- predict;
- explain.

AI shall not inherently:

- own Product Objects;
- approve Products;
- redefine Policies;
- bypass governance;
- modify Ownership.

Business accountability remains outside AI.

---

# AI Inputs

AI may consume:

- Product Objects;
- Product Relationships;
- Product Events;
- Product Policies;
- Product Lifecycles;
- Product documentation;
- enterprise knowledge.

Input availability depends upon applicable access controls.

---

# AI Outputs

AI may produce:

- recommendations;
- analyses;
- classifications;
- summaries;
- risk indicators;
- draft documentation.

AI outputs do not constitute authoritative Product data unless accepted through governed business processes.

---

# Human Governance

Organizations shall define governance determining:

- when AI may advise;
- when human approval is required;
- when AI-generated content may be accepted;
- how AI recommendations are reviewed;
- how accountability is maintained.

AI shall remain subject to enterprise governance.

---

# Explainability

Organizations should ensure AI-supported Product decisions remain explainable.

Explainability may include:

- reasoning summaries;
- supporting evidence;
- referenced Product Objects;
- applied Policies;
- confidence indicators.

Implementation mechanisms remain outside the scope of this specification.

---

# AI Governance

AI participation shall comply with:

- Product Policies;
- AI Policies;
- enterprise governance;
- applicable regulations;
- security requirements.

AI governance shall preserve trust, transparency, and accountability.

---

# AI Evolution

Product AI Capabilities may evolve through:

- improved analytical models;
- expanded commercial knowledge;
- enhanced recommendation quality;
- compatibility-preserving refinements.

AI evolution shall not modify Product semantics defined by this specification.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Product AI builds upon:

- AI
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Policy
- Event
- Capability
- Domain Governance

Additional AI specifications define enterprise-wide AI behavior.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- foundation models;
- machine learning algorithms;
- vector databases;
- inference platforms;
- implementation technologies.

AI participation remains implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Product implementation shall:

- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- ensure AI traceability;
- maintain human accountability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
