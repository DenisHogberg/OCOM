# Product Key Performance Indicators

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-PRODUCT-KPI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) applicable to the Product Domain.

Product KPIs provide standardized measures for evaluating the effectiveness, quality, maturity, and governance of Product Objects and Product Capabilities.

KPIs support commercial governance, portfolio management, continuous improvement, and enterprise interoperability.

---

# Definition

A Product KPI is a measurable business indicator used to evaluate the performance of the Product Domain.

KPIs measure commercial management outcomes rather than customer behavior, financial performance, or implementation technologies.

---

# Business Meaning

Product KPIs provide objective insight into how effectively the enterprise defines, governs, evolves, and maintains its commercial offerings.

They support strategic product management and portfolio optimization.

---

# Design Principles

Product KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- align with Product Capabilities;
- remain objectively measurable;
- preserve semantic consistency;
- support historical comparison;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

Product KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

## Product Portfolio Performance

Measures evaluating the enterprise product portfolio.

Examples include:

- active Products;
- retired Products;
- portfolio growth rate;
- portfolio stability.

---

## Product Lifecycle Performance

Measures evaluating Product Lifecycles.

Examples include:

- time to publication;
- approval cycle time;
- lifecycle transition rate;
- retirement completion rate.

---

## Offering Performance

Measures evaluating commercial Offerings.

Examples include:

- published Offerings;
- offering availability;
- offering revision frequency;
- offering approval rate.

---

## Subscription Performance

Measures evaluating Subscription structures.

Examples include:

- active Subscription Plans;
- subscription plan revisions;
- plan retirement rate;
- plan availability.

---

## Bundle Performance

Measures evaluating Bundle management.

Examples include:

- active Bundles;
- bundle utilization;
- bundle revision rate;
- bundle consistency.

---

## Configuration Performance

Measures evaluating configurable Products.

Examples include:

- configuration completeness;
- configuration validation rate;
- feature reuse;
- configuration consistency.

---

## Governance Performance

Measures evaluating Product governance.

Examples include:

- policy compliance rate;
- review completion rate;
- governance exceptions;
- product quality index.

---

# KPI Ownership

Every KPI shall have an owning Domain.

The Product Domain owns KPIs evaluating Product Objects and Product Capabilities.

Ownership of a KPI does not imply ownership of referenced Objects.

---

# KPI Calculation

Organizations shall define calculation methods appropriate to their commercial environment.

Calculation methods may include:

- counts;
- percentages;
- ratios;
- trend analysis;
- duration measurements.

Calculation formulas are implementation-specific.

---

# KPI Interpretation

KPIs shall be interpreted within their commercial context.

Interpretation may consider:

- portfolio strategy;
- market objectives;
- product governance;
- historical performance;
- applicable Policies.

Thresholds and performance targets are organization-specific.

---

# KPI Governance

Product KPIs shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- business definitions;
- calculation rules;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance ensures enterprise-wide commercial consistency.

---

# KPI Evolution

Product KPIs may evolve through:

- additional indicators;
- refined business definitions;
- improved calculation methods;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Breaking semantic changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Product KPIs build upon:

- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Domain
- Policy
- Governance

Additional Product specifications define the business context in which KPIs are evaluated.

---

# Independence

The Product KPI specification does not prescribe:

- product analytics platforms;
- BI tools;
- reporting software;
- implementation technologies.

KPIs remain logical business measures independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Product implementation shall:

- define measurable Product KPIs;
- assign KPI Ownership;
- provide consistent KPI definitions;
- govern KPI evolution;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
