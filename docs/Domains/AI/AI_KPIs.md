# AI KPIs

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-AI-KPIS-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) used by the AI Domain.

AI KPIs measure the effectiveness, quality, governance, explainability, reliability, and business impact of enterprise AI while preserving Entity Ownership and enterprise interoperability.

KPIs evaluate AI business performance rather than implementation-specific technical metrics.

---

# Definition

An AI KPI is a measurable business indicator that evaluates the performance of AI Entities, AI Processes, and AI Capabilities.

AI KPIs support governance, optimization, risk management, and continuous improvement.

Metric definitions are governed by the Core KPI specification.

---

# Business Meaning

AI KPIs provide objective visibility into enterprise AI operations.

They enable organizations to evaluate whether AI produces trustworthy, explainable, timely, and valuable outcomes while operating within approved governance boundaries.

---

# Design Principles

AI KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- remain objectively measurable;
- support enterprise governance;
- preserve Entity Ownership;
- remain comparable over time;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

AI KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Effectiveness KPIs

Effectiveness KPIs evaluate AI usefulness.

Examples include:

- Recommendation Acceptance Rate;
- Decision Proposal Acceptance Rate;
- Business Outcome Improvement;
- Recommendation Precision;
- Recommendation Recall;
- AI Success Rate.

Effectiveness KPIs measure business value delivered by AI.

---

## Quality KPIs

Quality KPIs evaluate AI output quality.

Examples include:

- Response Quality Score;
- Insight Accuracy;
- Recommendation Accuracy;
- Hallucination Rate;
- Consistency Score;
- Validation Success Rate.

Quality KPIs measure AI reliability.

---

## Explainability KPIs

Explainability KPIs evaluate transparency.

Examples include:

- Explainability Coverage;
- Reasoning Trace Availability;
- Evidence Coverage;
- Source Attribution Rate;
- Explanation Completeness.

Explainability KPIs measure transparency of AI behavior.

---

## Governance KPIs

Governance KPIs evaluate compliance.

Examples include:

- Policy Compliance Rate;
- Approval Completion Rate;
- Governance Exception Rate;
- Safety Assessment Coverage;
- Human Review Rate.

Governance KPIs measure trustworthy AI operation.

---

## Operational KPIs

Operational KPIs evaluate AI execution.

Examples include:

- AI Task Completion Rate;
- Workflow Completion Rate;
- Tool Invocation Success Rate;
- Average Response Time;
- Session Completion Rate.

Operational KPIs measure AI execution performance.

---

## Knowledge KPIs

Knowledge KPIs evaluate enterprise knowledge utilization.

Examples include:

- Evidence Utilization Rate;
- Context Relevance Score;
- Knowledge Coverage;
- Source Freshness;
- Source Reliability Score.

Knowledge KPIs measure the quality of information supporting AI decisions.

---

## Collaboration KPIs

Collaboration KPIs evaluate AI interaction with enterprise participants.

Examples include:

- Human Acceptance Rate;
- Collaboration Success Rate;
- Recommendation Adoption Rate;
- User Satisfaction;
- Feedback Completion Rate.

Collaboration KPIs measure AI integration into enterprise workflows.

---

## Improvement KPIs

Improvement KPIs evaluate organizational learning.

Examples include:

- Prompt Improvement Rate;
- Workflow Optimization Rate;
- Feedback Resolution Rate;
- Continuous Improvement Index;
- AI Capability Growth.

Improvement KPIs measure AI maturity.

---

# KPI Ownership

The AI Domain owns KPIs evaluating AI Entities, AI Processes, and AI Capabilities.

Business KPIs owned by other Domains remain under their respective ownership.

Cross-Domain dashboards may combine KPIs without transferring ownership.

---

# KPI Governance

AI KPIs shall be governed through:

- standardized definitions;
- approved calculation methods;
- consistent measurement;
- auditability;
- organizational governance.

Organizations shall preserve KPI semantic stability over time.

---

# KPI Evolution

AI KPIs may evolve through:

- additional business measurements;
- refined calculation methods;
- improved governance;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Historical KPI meaning shall remain preserved.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

AI KPIs build upon:

- KPI
- Capability
- Process
- Lifecycle
- Event
- AI Governance

Additional specifications define enterprise-wide AI measurement and reporting.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- BI platforms;
- observability systems;
- AI monitoring tools;
- LLM providers;
- implementation technologies.

AI KPIs remain implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming implementation shall:

- define standardized AI KPIs;
- preserve KPI consistency;
- support objective measurement;
- support enterprise interoperability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
