# Human Resources Key Performance Indicators

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-HR-KPI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) applicable to the Human Resources (HR) Domain.

HR KPIs provide standardized business measures for evaluating workforce effectiveness, organizational capability, workforce development, governance, and operational maturity.

KPIs enable organizations to continuously improve workforce management while preserving Domain Ownership and enterprise interoperability.

---

# Definition

An HR KPI is a measurable business indicator used to evaluate the performance of HR Entities, HR Processes, and HR Capabilities.

HR KPIs measure workforce outcomes rather than payroll processing, identity management, or technical HR systems.

KPI semantics are defined by the Core KPI specification.

---

# Business Meaning

HR KPIs provide objective insight into how effectively the organization recruits, develops, organizes, retains, and supports its workforce.

KPIs support strategic workforce planning, operational excellence, governance, and continuous organizational improvement.

---

# Design Principles

HR KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- align with HR Capabilities;
- remain objectively measurable;
- preserve semantic consistency;
- support historical comparison;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

HR KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Workforce Performance

Measures evaluating workforce health.

Examples include:

- active Employees;
- workforce growth rate;
- employee retention rate;
- employee turnover rate;
- average employment duration.

---

## Organizational Performance

Measures evaluating organizational effectiveness.

Examples include:

- position fulfillment rate;
- organizational capacity utilization;
- department stability;
- organizational restructuring frequency;
- reporting structure consistency.

---

## Recruitment Performance

Measures evaluating workforce acquisition.

Examples include:

- hiring cycle time;
- hiring request completion rate;
- position fill rate;
- recruitment efficiency;
- onboarding completion rate.

---

## Competency Performance

Measures evaluating workforce capability.

Examples include:

- competency coverage;
- certification completion rate;
- skill validation rate;
- qualification maturity;
- competency gap index.

---

## Learning and Development

Measures evaluating workforce development.

Examples include:

- training completion rate;
- development plan completion;
- career progression rate;
- mentorship participation;
- learning effectiveness.

---

## Performance Management

Measures evaluating workforce performance.

Examples include:

- goal completion rate;
- performance review completion;
- feedback coverage;
- performance improvement rate;
- development recommendation completion.

---

## Workforce Planning

Measures evaluating strategic workforce planning.

Examples include:

- workforce forecast accuracy;
- succession readiness;
- hiring priority completion;
- organizational capacity readiness;
- workforce planning maturity.

---

## Governance Performance

Measures evaluating HR governance.

Examples include:

- policy compliance rate;
- workforce audit completion;
- documentation completeness;
- governance review completion;
- governance exception rate.

---

# KPI Ownership

Every KPI shall have one owning Domain.

The HR Domain owns KPIs evaluating workforce Entities, Processes, and Capabilities.

Ownership of a KPI does not imply ownership of referenced Objects.

---

# KPI Calculation

Organizations shall define calculation methods appropriate to their workforce environment.

Calculation methods may include:

- counts;
- percentages;
- ratios;
- duration measurements;
- trend analysis;
- maturity scoring.

Calculation formulas are implementation-specific.

---

# KPI Interpretation

KPIs shall be interpreted within their business context.

Interpretation may consider:

- organizational strategy;
- workforce composition;
- business objectives;
- historical performance;
- applicable Policies.

Performance targets remain organization-specific.

---

# KPI Governance

HR KPIs shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- business definitions;
- calculation rules;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance ensures enterprise-wide consistency.

---

# KPI Evolution

HR KPIs may evolve through:

- additional indicators;
- refined business definitions;
- improved calculation methods;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Breaking semantic changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

HR KPIs build upon:

- KPI
- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Domain
- Policy
- Governance

Additional HR specifications define the business context in which KPIs are evaluated.

---

# Independence

The HR KPI specification does not prescribe:

- HR analytics platforms;
- dashboards;
- reporting software;
- workforce management tools;
- implementation technologies.

KPIs remain logical business measures independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming HR implementation shall:

- define measurable HR KPIs;
- assign KPI Ownership;
- provide consistent KPI definitions;
- govern KPI evolution;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
