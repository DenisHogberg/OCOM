# Human Resources Policies

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-HR-POLICIES-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Policies governing the Human Resources (HR) Domain.

HR Policies establish business rules for managing Employees, Employment, Organizational Structure, Positions, Competencies, Development, and workforce governance while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, enterprise interoperability, and accountability.

Policies ensure workforce management remains consistent, transparent, fair, and auditable.

---

# Definition

An HR Policy is a business rule that constrains, authorizes, or guides behavior within the Human Resources Domain.

Policies govern HR Entities, Processes, Capabilities, Relationships, and Events independently of implementation technologies.

Policy semantics are defined by the Core Policy specification.

---

# Business Meaning

HR Policies define how organizations recruit, employ, develop, evaluate, organize, and retain their workforce.

They establish standardized governance for workforce management while supporting organizational consistency and long-term sustainability.

---

# Design Principles

HR Policies shall:

- define explicit business rules;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- support auditability;
- remain version controlled;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Policy Scope

HR Policies may govern:

- HR Entities;
- Lifecycles;
- Relationships;
- Processes;
- Capabilities;
- Events;
- AI-assisted workforce activities.

Policies may also govern interactions with other Domains.

---

# Policy Categories

HR Policies may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Employment Policies

Policies governing employment relationships.

Examples include:

- hiring eligibility;
- employment approval;
- probation management;
- employment modification;
- leave management;
- employment termination.

---

## Organizational Policies

Policies governing organizational structure.

Examples include:

- department creation;
- organizational restructuring;
- reporting relationships;
- position authorization;
- organizational responsibilities.

---

## Position Policies

Policies governing organizational responsibilities.

Examples include:

- Position approval;
- Position classification;
- Position assignment;
- authority delegation;
- responsibility definition.

---

## Competency Policies

Policies governing workforce capabilities.

Examples include:

- competency requirements;
- certification requirements;
- qualification validation;
- skill maintenance;
- competency review.

---

## Learning and Development Policies

Policies governing workforce development.

Examples include:

- training participation;
- mandatory learning;
- career development;
- mentorship participation;
- development planning.

---

## Performance Policies

Policies governing workforce evaluation.

Examples include:

- goal definition;
- review frequency;
- feedback standards;
- performance evaluation;
- development recommendations.

---

## Workforce Planning Policies

Policies governing strategic workforce management.

Examples include:

- workforce planning;
- succession planning;
- hiring prioritization;
- organizational capacity planning;
- workforce optimization.

---

## Governance Policies

Policies governing enterprise workforce management.

Examples include:

- documentation standards;
- audit requirements;
- policy compliance;
- governance reviews;
- exception management.

---

# Policy Application

Policies may apply to:

- individual HR Entities;
- Object categories;
- Processes;
- Capabilities;
- Relationships;
- Events.

Policy applicability shall be explicitly defined.

---

# Policy Enforcement

Organizations shall establish mechanisms for enforcing HR Policies.

Enforcement mechanisms are implementation-specific and outside the scope of this specification.

Policy enforcement may result in:

- Lifecycle transitions;
- HR Events;
- approvals;
- workforce reviews;
- governance actions.

---

# Policy Governance

HR Policies shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- approval;
- publication;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance shall ensure enterprise-wide consistency.

---

# Policy Evolution

HR Policies may evolve through:

- organizational improvements;
- governance refinements;
- workforce experience;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Breaking Policy changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

HR Policies build upon:

- Policy
- Domain Governance
- Capability
- Lifecycle
- Object
- Contract
- Constraint

Additional HR specifications define where individual Policies apply.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- HR software;
- payroll systems;
- workflow engines;
- rule engines;
- implementation technologies.

Policies remain logical business rules independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming HR implementation shall:

- define explicit HR Policies;
- assign Policy Ownership;
- govern Policy lifecycles;
- support Policy traceability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
