# Human Resources AI

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-HR-AI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Artificial Intelligence (AI) participates within the Human Resources (HR) Domain.

AI assists workforce management by improving recruitment, organizational planning, competency analysis, workforce development, performance evaluation, and decision support while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, governance, and human accountability.

AI participation remains advisory unless explicitly authorized by applicable Policies.

---

# Definition

HR AI represents the application of AI Capabilities to HR Entities, Processes, Relationships, Events, Policies, and workforce knowledge.

AI operates within enterprise governance and does not become the owner of workforce information or organizational decisions.

AI semantics are defined by the AI specifications of the OCOM framework.

---

# Business Meaning

AI enables organizations to better understand, develop, organize, and support their workforce by providing intelligent recommendations, predictive insights, semantic understanding, and operational assistance.

AI augments workforce management while preserving transparency, accountability, and organizational governance.

---

# Design Principles

AI participation within the HR Domain shall:

- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- operate within applicable Policies;
- produce traceable outcomes;
- support explainability;
- remain human-governed;
- remain technology independent.

---

# AI Capability Categories

AI may support the following HR activities.

---

## Workforce Intelligence

Examples include:

- workforce segmentation;
- organizational analysis;
- workforce capacity assessment;
- retention risk estimation;
- workforce trend analysis.

---

## Recruitment Intelligence

Examples include:

- candidate matching;
- Position suitability analysis;
- hiring prioritization;
- competency gap analysis;
- workforce demand forecasting.

AI recommendations shall not constitute hiring decisions.

---

## Organizational Intelligence

Examples include:

- organizational structure analysis;
- reporting structure optimization;
- workload balancing;
- organizational efficiency assessment;
- restructuring impact analysis.

---

## Competency Intelligence

Examples include:

- Skill matching;
- competency recommendations;
- learning recommendations;
- certification analysis;
- capability gap identification.

---

## Development Intelligence

Examples include:

- personalized learning recommendations;
- Career Path suggestions;
- mentorship recommendations;
- development prioritization;
- workforce growth analysis.

---

## Performance Intelligence

Examples include:

- performance trend analysis;
- Goal alignment analysis;
- development opportunity identification;
- workforce benchmarking;
- performance forecasting.

AI shall not replace formal Performance Reviews.

---

## Governance Assistance

Examples include:

- Policy validation;
- documentation review;
- workforce audit support;
- organizational consistency analysis;
- governance monitoring.

---

## Knowledge Assistance

Examples include:

- semantic search;
- workforce summarization;
- organizational knowledge discovery;
- document generation;
- operational guidance.

---

# AI Decision Model

AI may:

- recommend;
- classify;
- summarize;
- analyze;
- predict;
- explain.

AI shall not inherently:

- hire Employees;
- terminate Employment;
- approve Positions;
- modify Organizational Structure;
- change Object Ownership;
- replace accountable management decisions.

Business accountability remains outside AI.

---

# AI Inputs

AI may consume:

- HR Entities;
- HR Relationships;
- HR Events;
- HR Policies;
- HR Lifecycles;
- workforce documentation;
- enterprise business knowledge.

Input availability depends upon applicable access controls.

---

# AI Outputs

AI may produce:

- recommendations;
- analyses;
- classifications;
- summaries;
- predictions;
- workforce insights;
- organizational risk indicators;
- supporting documentation.

AI outputs do not constitute authoritative HR data unless accepted through governed business processes.

---

# Human Governance

Organizations shall define governance determining:

- when AI may advise;
- when human approval is required;
- when AI recommendations may be accepted;
- how AI analyses are reviewed;
- how accountability is maintained.

AI shall remain subject to enterprise governance.

---

# Explainability

Organizations should ensure AI-supported HR decisions remain explainable.

Explainability may include:

- reasoning summaries;
- supporting evidence;
- referenced HR Entities;
- applied Policies;
- confidence indicators.

Implementation mechanisms remain outside the scope of this specification.

---

# AI Governance

AI participation shall comply with:

- HR Policies;
- AI Policies;
- enterprise governance;
- applicable regulations;
- security requirements.

AI governance shall preserve trust, transparency, fairness, and accountability.

---

# AI Evolution

HR AI Capabilities may evolve through:

- improved analytical models;
- expanded organizational knowledge;
- enhanced recommendation quality;
- compatibility-preserving refinements.

AI evolution shall not modify HR semantics defined by this specification.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

HR AI builds upon:

- AI
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Policy
- Event
- Capability
- Domain Governance

Additional AI specifications define enterprise-wide AI behavior.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- foundation models;
- machine learning algorithms;
- HR platforms;
- recommendation engines;
- vector databases;
- implementation technologies.

HR AI remains implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming HR implementation shall:

- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- ensure AI traceability;
- maintain human accountability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
