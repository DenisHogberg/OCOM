# Human Resources Capabilities

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-HR-CAPABILITIES-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the business Capabilities owned by the Human Resources (HR) Domain.

HR Capabilities represent the business functions required to manage the enterprise workforce while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, governance, and enterprise interoperability.

Capabilities define what the HR Domain is able to perform rather than how those functions are implemented.

---

# Definition

An HR Capability is a business function owned by the Human Resources Domain.

Capabilities operate on HR Entities according to applicable Policies and Lifecycles while producing governed business outcomes.

Capability semantics are defined by the Core Capability specification.

---

# Business Meaning

HR Capabilities enable organizations to recruit, organize, develop, evaluate, and retain their workforce through standardized business functions.

They provide a consistent operational model independent of organizational structure, HR software, or administrative procedures.

---

# Design Principles

HR Capabilities shall:

- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- operate according to Policies;
- produce governed Events;
- support enterprise interoperability;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Capability Categories

HR Capabilities may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Workforce Management

Capabilities supporting workforce administration.

Examples include:

- establish Employment;
- update Employment;
- assign Position;
- transfer Employee;
- suspend Employment;
- conclude Employment.

---

## Organizational Management

Capabilities supporting organizational structure.

Examples include:

- establish Department;
- establish Team;
- manage Organizational Units;
- manage reporting structure;
- reorganize organization;
- retire organizational entities.

---

## Position Management

Capabilities supporting organizational responsibilities.

Examples include:

- create Position;
- approve Position;
- update Position;
- assign Position;
- retire Position;
- manage Responsibilities.

---

## Competency Management

Capabilities supporting workforce competencies.

Examples include:

- define Competency;
- validate Skill;
- manage Certification;
- assess Qualification;
- maintain Competency Profile.

---

## Learning and Development

Capabilities supporting workforce growth.

Examples include:

- assign Training;
- manage Development Plan;
- maintain Career Path;
- coordinate Mentorship;
- evaluate learning outcomes.

---

## Performance Management

Capabilities supporting workforce evaluation.

Examples include:

- define Goal;
- perform Performance Review;
- record Feedback;
- evaluate Performance;
- recommend Development Actions.

---

## Workforce Planning

Capabilities supporting strategic workforce management.

Examples include:

- create Workforce Plan;
- forecast Workforce Capacity;
- manage Succession Plan;
- prioritize Hiring;
- analyze organizational capability.

---

## Governance Management

Capabilities supporting HR governance.

Examples include:

- validate HR Policy compliance;
- coordinate Workforce Audit;
- maintain HR documentation;
- govern organizational changes;
- enforce workforce standards.

---

# Capability Consumers

HR Capabilities may be consumed by:

- Product;
- Support;
- Marketing;
- Affiliate;
- Finance;
- Compliance;
- BI;
- AI;
- other enterprise Domains.

Capability consumption does not transfer Object Ownership.

---

# Capability Governance

HR Capabilities shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- Policies;
- Contracts;
- Constraints;
- version management.

Governance shall ensure consistent enterprise workforce management.

---

# Capability Evolution

HR Capabilities may evolve through:

- additional business functions;
- expanded interoperability;
- refined governance;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Capability evolution shall preserve business semantics.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

HR Capabilities build upon:

- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Process
- Event
- Policy
- Domain Governance

Additional HR specifications define how individual Capabilities interact with HR Entities.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- HR platforms;
- recruitment systems;
- payroll software;
- workflow engines;
- implementation technologies.

HR Capabilities remain implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming HR implementation shall:

- define standardized HR Capabilities;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- govern Capability evolution;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
