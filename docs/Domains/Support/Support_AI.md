# Support AI

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-SUPPORT-AI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Artificial Intelligence (AI) participates within the Support Domain.

AI assists enterprise support by improving issue resolution, service quality, operational efficiency, knowledge discovery, and decision preparation while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, and human governance.

AI participation remains advisory unless explicitly authorized by applicable Policies.

---

# Definition

Support AI represents the application of AI Capabilities to Support Objects, Support Processes, Relationships, Events, Policies, and operational knowledge.

AI operates within enterprise governance and does not become the owner of Support Objects or business decisions.

AI semantics are defined by the AI specifications of the OCOM framework.

---

# Business Meaning

AI enables organizations to deliver faster, more consistent, and more informed support services by assisting support personnel throughout the service lifecycle.

AI augments operational decision-making while preserving accountability, traceability, and governance.

---

# Design Principles

AI participation within the Support Domain shall:

- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- operate within applicable Policies;
- produce traceable outcomes;
- support explainability;
- remain human-governed;
- remain technology independent.

---

AI may support the following Support activities.

- Case summarization;

# AI Decision Model

AI may:

- recommend;
- classify;
- summarize;
- analyze;
- predict;
- explain.

AI shall not inherently:

- approve Cases;
- close Incidents;
- authorize Service Requests;
- modify Support Policies;
- change Object Ownership;
- replace accountable business decisions.

Business accountability remains outside AI.

---

# AI Inputs

AI may consume:

- Support Objects;
- Support Relationships;
- Support Events;
- Support Policies;
- Support Lifecycles;
- Knowledge Articles;
- enterprise operational knowledge.

Input availability depends upon applicable access controls.

---

# AI Outputs

AI may produce:

- recommendations;
- analyses;
- classifications;
- summaries;
- predictions;
- risk indicators;
- draft Knowledge Articles;
- supporting documentation.

AI outputs do not constitute authoritative Support data unless accepted through governed business processes.

---

# Human Governance

Organizations shall define governance determining:

- when AI may advise;
- when human approval is required;
- when AI-generated knowledge may be accepted;
- how AI recommendations are reviewed;
- how accountability is maintained.

AI shall remain subject to enterprise governance.

---

# Explainability

Organizations should ensure AI-supported Support decisions remain explainable.

Explainability may include:

- reasoning summaries;
- supporting evidence;
- referenced Support Objects;
- applied Policies;
- confidence indicators.

Implementation mechanisms remain outside the scope of this specification.

---

# AI Governance

AI participation shall comply with:

- Support Policies;
- AI Policies;
- enterprise governance;
- applicable regulations;
- security requirements.

AI governance shall preserve trust, transparency, and accountability.

---

# AI Evolution

Support AI Capabilities may evolve through:

- improved analytical models;
- expanded organizational knowledge;
- enhanced recommendation quality;
- compatibility-preserving refinements.

AI evolution shall not modify Support semantics defined by this specification.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Support AI builds upon:

- AI
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Policy
- Event
- Capability
- Domain Governance

Additional AI specifications define enterprise-wide AI behavior.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- foundation models;
- machine learning algorithms;
- service desk platforms;
- vector databases;
- inference platforms;
- implementation technologies.

Support AI remains implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Support implementation shall:

- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- ensure AI traceability;
- maintain human accountability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
