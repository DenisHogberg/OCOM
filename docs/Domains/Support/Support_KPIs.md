# Support Key Performance Indicators

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-SUPPORT-KPI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) applicable to the Support Domain.

Support KPIs provide standardized business measures for evaluating the effectiveness, efficiency, quality, governance, and maturity of enterprise support operations.

KPIs enable organizations to continuously improve service delivery while preserving Domain Ownership and enterprise interoperability.

---

# Definition

A Support KPI is a measurable business indicator used to evaluate the performance of Support Objects, Support Processes, and Support Capabilities.

Support KPIs measure business outcomes owned by the Support Domain rather than customer relationships, financial performance, or technical infrastructure.

KPI semantics are defined by the Core KPI specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Support KPIs provide objective insight into how effectively the enterprise delivers customer assistance, resolves operational issues, manages service requests, and improves organizational knowledge.

KPIs support operational excellence, governance, and continuous service improvement.

---

# Design Principles

Support KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- align with Support Capabilities;
- remain objectively measurable;
- preserve semantic consistency;
- support historical comparison;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

Support KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Case Performance

Measures evaluating Case management.

Examples include:

- active Cases;
- Case resolution rate;
- Case closure rate;
- Case backlog;
- Case aging;
- Case reopening rate.

---

## Incident Performance

Measures evaluating Incident management.

Examples include:

- Incident resolution rate;
- Incident recurrence rate;
- Incident severity distribution;
- major Incident frequency;
- Incident lifecycle duration.

---

## Service Request Performance

Measures evaluating Service Requests.

Examples include:

- Service Request fulfillment rate;
- Service Request approval time;
- Request completion rate;
- Request backlog;
- Request processing time.

---

## Resolution Performance

Measures evaluating issue resolution.

Examples include:

- Resolution success rate;
- first-attempt Resolution rate;
- Root Cause Analysis completion rate;
- workaround utilization;
- Resolution verification rate.

---

## Knowledge Performance

Measures evaluating Knowledge management.

Examples include:

- Knowledge Article publication rate;
- Knowledge reuse rate;
- Knowledge freshness;
- article review completion rate;
- documentation coverage.

---

## Operational Performance

Measures evaluating support operations.

Examples include:

- queue utilization;
- assignment efficiency;
- workload distribution;
- operational responsiveness;
- support capacity utilization.

---

## Service Quality

Measures evaluating service quality.

Examples include:

- customer satisfaction;
- service quality index;
- resolution quality;
- repeat issue rate;
- support consistency.

---

## Governance Performance

Measures evaluating Support governance.

Examples include:

- Policy compliance rate;
- audit completion rate;
- approval turnaround time;
- governance exception rate;
- operational maturity index.

---

# KPI Ownership

Every KPI shall have an owning Domain.

The Support Domain owns KPIs evaluating Support Objects, Support Processes, and Support Capabilities.

Ownership of a KPI does not imply ownership of referenced Objects.

---

# KPI Calculation

Organizations shall define calculation methods appropriate to their support environment.

Calculation methods may include:

- counts;
- percentages;
- ratios;
- duration measurements;
- trend analysis;
- maturity scoring.

Calculation formulas are implementation-specific.

---

# KPI Interpretation

KPIs shall be interpreted within their business context.

Interpretation may consider:

- service objectives;
- organizational priorities;
- support policies;
- historical performance;
- operational environment.

Performance targets remain organization-specific.

---

# KPI Governance

Support KPIs shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- business definitions;
- calculation rules;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance ensures enterprise-wide consistency.

---

# KPI Evolution

Support KPIs may evolve through:

- additional indicators;
- refined business definitions;
- improved calculation methods;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Breaking semantic changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Support KPIs build upon:

- KPI
- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Domain
- Policy
- Governance

Additional Support specifications define the business context in which KPIs are evaluated.

---

# Independence

The Support KPI specification does not prescribe:

- reporting platforms;
- dashboards;
- service desk software;
- analytics tools;
- implementation technologies.

KPIs remain logical business measures independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Support implementation shall:

- define measurable Support KPIs;
- assign KPI Ownership;
- provide consistent KPI definitions;
- govern KPI evolution;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
