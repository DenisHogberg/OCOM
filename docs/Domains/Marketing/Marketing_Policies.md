# Marketing Policies

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-MARKETING-POLICIES-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Policies governing the Marketing Domain.

Marketing Policies establish business rules governing Marketing Objects, Processes, Capabilities, Events, and market communication throughout the enterprise.

Policies ensure Marketing activities are consistent, controlled, traceable, and aligned with enterprise objectives.

---

# Definition

A Marketing Policy is a business rule that constrains, authorizes, or guides behavior within the Marketing Domain.

Policies define expected marketing behavior independently of marketing automation platforms, advertising systems, or implementation technologies.

Policy semantics are defined by the Core Policy specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Marketing Policies establish the governance framework for enterprise market communication.

They ensure Campaigns, Brands, Audiences, Content, Channels, and Promotions evolve consistently throughout their Lifecycles while preserving interoperability with other Domains.

---

# Design Principles

Marketing Policies shall:

- define explicit business rules;
- support Marketing governance;
- preserve business consistency;
- remain auditable;
- be version controlled;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Policy Scope

Marketing Policies may apply to:

- Marketing Objects;
- Relationships;
- Lifecycles;
- Events;
- Processes;
- Capabilities;
- AI-assisted Marketing activities.

Policies may also govern interactions with other Domains.

---

# Policy Categories

Marketing Policies may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Campaign Policies

Policies governing Campaign management.

Examples include:

- campaign approval;
- campaign ownership;
- campaign publication;
- campaign retirement;
- campaign scheduling.

---

## Brand Policies

Policies governing Brand management.

Examples include:

- brand governance;
- brand identity standards;
- brand asset approval;
- brand publication;
- brand retirement.

---

## Audience Policies

Policies governing Audience management.

Examples include:

- audience definition;
- segmentation standards;
- persona governance;
- audience eligibility;
- audience maintenance.

---

## Content Policies

Policies governing marketing communication.

Examples include:

- content approval;
- content publication;
- content versioning;
- content archival;
- creative review.

---

## Channel Policies

Policies governing communication channels.

Examples include:

- channel eligibility;

---

## Promotion Policies

Policies governing Promotions.

Examples include:

- promotion approval;

Policies governing enterprise marketing operations.

Policies governing market communication.

---

# Policy Application

Policies may apply to:

- individual Marketing Objects;
- Object categories;
- Capabilities;
- Processes;
- Events;
- Relationships.

Policy applicability shall be explicitly defined.

---

# Policy Enforcement

Organizations shall establish mechanisms for enforcing Marketing Policies.

Enforcement mechanisms are implementation-specific and outside the scope of this specification.

Policy enforcement may result in:

- Lifecycle transitions;
- Marketing Events;
- approvals;
- governance reviews;
- publication decisions.

---

# Policy Governance

Marketing Policies shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- approval;
- publication;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance shall ensure enterprise-wide consistency.

---

# Policy Evolution

Marketing Policies may evolve through:

- business strategy changes;
- market evolution;
- governance improvements;
- compatibility-preserving refinements.

Breaking Policy changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Marketing Policies build upon:

- Policy
- Domain Governance
- Capability
- Lifecycle
- Object
- Contract
- Constraint

Additional Marketing specifications define where individual Policies apply.

---

# Independence

The Marketing Policies specification does not prescribe:

- marketing automation platforms;
- campaign software;
- workflow engines;
- advertising systems;
- implementation technologies.

Policies remain logical business rules independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Marketing implementation shall:

- define explicit Marketing Policies;
- assign Policy Ownership;
- govern Policy lifecycles;
- support Policy traceability;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
