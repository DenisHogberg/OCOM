# Marketing Processes

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-MARKETING-PROCESSES-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Processes managed by the Marketing Domain.

Marketing Processes coordinate Marketing Objects to deliver consistent market communication while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, and enterprise interoperability.

Processes describe business coordination independently of implementation technologies.

---

# Definition

A Marketing Process is a governed sequence of business activities coordinating Marketing Objects to achieve a marketing objective.

Processes coordinate Marketing Objects but do not own their Lifecycles.

Process semantics are defined by the Core Process specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Marketing Processes enable organizations to consistently plan, execute, govern, evaluate, and improve enterprise marketing activities.

Processes ensure marketing operations remain repeatable, measurable, and aligned with business strategy.

---

# Design Principles

Marketing Processes shall:

- coordinate Marketing Objects;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- produce governed Events;
- support enterprise interoperability;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Process Ownership

Each Marketing Process shall have an owning Domain.

The Marketing Domain owns Processes coordinating Marketing Objects.

Operational Processes owned by other Domains remain outside the scope of the Marketing Domain.

---

# Process Coordination

Marketing Processes may coordinate:

- Marketing Objects;
- Relationships;
- Events;
- Policies;
- marketing strategies;
- business goals.

Processes may reference Objects owned by other Domains without assuming Ownership.

---

# Process Outcomes

Marketing Processes may produce:

- Marketing Events;
- updated Marketing Objects;
- approved Campaigns;
- published Content;
- audience definitions;
- governance decisions;
- marketing plans.

Business outcomes remain governed by applicable Policies.

---

# Process Evolution

Marketing Processes may evolve through:

- improved marketing practices;
- additional business activities;
- compatibility-preserving refinements;
- expanded enterprise integration.

Breaking Process changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Marketing Processes build upon:

- Process
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Event
- Capability
- Policy
- Domain Governance

Additional Marketing specifications define the Objects coordinated by these Processes.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- marketing automation platforms;
- workflow engines;
- campaign management software;
- implementation technologies.

Processes remain logical business coordination models independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Marketing implementation shall:

- define governed Marketing Processes;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- support standardized Process coordination;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
