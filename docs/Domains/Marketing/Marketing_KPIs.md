# Marketing Key Performance Indicators

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-MARKETING-KPI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) applicable to the Marketing Domain.

Marketing KPIs provide standardized measures for evaluating the effectiveness, quality, maturity, governance, and operational performance of Marketing Objects and Marketing Capabilities.

KPIs support strategic decision-making, continuous improvement, and enterprise interoperability.

---

# Definition

A Marketing KPI is a measurable business indicator used to evaluate the performance of the Marketing Domain.

Marketing KPIs measure marketing activities and business outcomes owned by the Marketing Domain rather than customer relationships, financial performance, or implementation technologies.

KPI semantics are defined by the Core KPI specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Marketing KPIs provide objective insight into how effectively the enterprise communicates commercial value, manages market presence, executes campaigns, governs marketing assets, and achieves strategic marketing objectives.

KPIs support operational excellence and long-term marketing effectiveness.

---

# Design Principles

Marketing KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- align with Marketing Capabilities;
- remain objectively measurable;
- preserve semantic consistency;
- support historical comparison;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

Marketing KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Campaign Performance

Measures evaluating Campaign execution.

Examples include:

- active Campaigns;
- Campaign completion rate;
- Campaign approval cycle time;
- Campaign launch success rate;
- Campaign execution consistency.

---

## Brand Performance

Measures evaluating Brand management.

Examples include:

- Brand consistency index;
- Brand asset utilization;
- Brand governance compliance;
- Brand publication rate;
- Brand portfolio coverage.

---

## Audience Performance

Measures evaluating Audience management.

Examples include:

- Audience growth;
- Audience coverage;
- Audience segmentation quality;
- Persona completeness;
- Audience maintenance rate.

---

## Content Performance

Measures evaluating marketing content.

Examples include:

- Content publication rate;
- Content approval cycle time;
- Content reuse rate;
- Content freshness;
- Creative production time.

---

## Channel Performance

Measures evaluating communication channels.

Examples include:

- active Channels;
- Channel utilization;
- Channel availability;
- Channel effectiveness;
- Channel consistency.

---

## Promotion Performance

Measures evaluating Promotions.

Examples include:

- active Promotions;
- Promotion completion rate;
- Promotion approval cycle;
- Promotion effectiveness;
- Promotion lifecycle duration.

---

## Governance Performance

Measures evaluating Marketing governance.

Examples include:

- Policy compliance rate;
- governance review completion;
- approval turnaround time;
- governance exception rate;
- marketing quality index.

---

## Operational Performance

Measures evaluating Marketing operations.

Examples include:

- marketing process efficiency;
- planning accuracy;
- marketing responsiveness;
- operational maturity;
- capability utilization.

---

# KPI Ownership

Every KPI shall have an owning Domain.

The Marketing Domain owns KPIs evaluating Marketing Objects and Marketing Capabilities.

Ownership of a KPI does not imply ownership of referenced Objects.

---

# KPI Calculation

Organizations shall define calculation methods appropriate to their marketing environment.

Calculation methods may include:

- counts;
- percentages;
- ratios;
- duration measurements;
- trend analysis;
- maturity scoring.

Calculation formulas are implementation-specific.

---

# KPI Interpretation

KPIs shall be interpreted within their business context.

Interpretation may consider:

- marketing strategy;
- market conditions;
- organizational objectives;
- historical performance;
- applicable Policies.

Performance targets remain organization-specific.

---

# KPI Governance

Marketing KPIs shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- business definitions;
- calculation rules;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance ensures enterprise-wide consistency.

---

# KPI Evolution

Marketing KPIs may evolve through:

- additional indicators;
- refined business definitions;
- improved calculation methods;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Breaking semantic changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Marketing KPIs build upon:

- KPI
- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Domain
- Policy
- Governance

Additional Marketing specifications define the business context in which KPIs are evaluated.

---

# Independence

The Marketing KPI specification does not prescribe:

- marketing analytics platforms;
- reporting tools;
- dashboards;
- business intelligence software;
- implementation technologies.

KPIs remain logical business measures independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Marketing implementation shall:

- define measurable Marketing KPIs;
- assign KPI Ownership;
- provide consistent KPI definitions;
- govern KPI evolution;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
