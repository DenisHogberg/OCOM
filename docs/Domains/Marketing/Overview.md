# Marketing Domain

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-MARKETING-README-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

The Marketing Domain owns enterprise market communication, brand presence, audience engagement, campaign management, and marketing content.

The Domain defines how the enterprise presents its Products and Services to the market while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, and enterprise interoperability.

Marketing enables organizations to communicate commercial value consistently across channels, audiences, and markets.

---

# Definition

The Marketing Domain is responsible for the planning, coordination, execution, and governance of enterprise marketing activities.

It owns Marketing Objects representing campaigns, brands, audiences, marketing assets, and communication initiatives.

Marketing does not own commercial transactions, customer relationships, financial records, or Product definitions.

---

# Business Meaning

Marketing transforms enterprise commercial offerings into market-facing communication.

It provides the capabilities required to position Products, engage target audiences, manage brand identity, and coordinate market activities while maintaining governance and consistency.

Marketing serves as the enterprise bridge between Product definition and Customer engagement.

---

# Domain Responsibilities

The Marketing Domain is responsible for:

- campaign management;
- audience management;
- brand management;
- marketing content management;
- creative asset management;
- marketing channel management;
- promotion management;
- marketing planning;
- marketing governance;
- market communication.

---

# Owned Entities

Typical Marketing Objects include:

- Campaign;
- Marketing Program;
- Brand;
- Brand Asset;
- Audience;
- Market Segment;
- Persona;
- Marketing Content;
- Creative Asset;
- Marketing Channel;
- Promotion;
- Marketing Calendar.

Ownership of Marketing Objects remains within the Marketing Domain.

---

# Domain Boundaries

The Marketing Domain owns market communication.

It does not own:

- Customers;
- Products;
- Payments;
- Financial records;
- Contracts;
- Compliance decisions.

These Objects remain owned by their respective Domains.

---

# Relationships with Other Domains

The Marketing Domain collaborates with multiple enterprise Domains.

Typical interactions include:

**Product**

Provides Products, Services, and commercial Offerings promoted through marketing activities.

**CRM**

Provides Customer interactions, customer journeys, and engagement outcomes.

**BI**

Provides analytical insight into marketing effectiveness.

**Finance**

Provides budgeting and financial reporting.

**Compliance**

Provides regulatory review and policy validation.

**AI**

Provides intelligent assistance supporting marketing activities.

Ownership of Objects is preserved across all Domain interactions.

---

# Design Principles

The Marketing Domain shall:

- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- coordinate market communication;
- support enterprise interoperability;
- emit governed Events;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Domain Governance

The Marketing Domain shall define governance for:

- Marketing Objects;
- Marketing Processes;
- Marketing Capabilities;
- Marketing Policies;
- Marketing Events;
- Marketing Lifecycles.

Governance shall ensure consistency, traceability, and accountability.

---

# Domain Evolution

The Marketing Domain may evolve through:

- additional Marketing Objects;
- expanded Capabilities;
- refined Processes;
- enhanced governance;
- compatibility-preserving improvements.

Evolution shall preserve interoperability with other OCOM Domains.

---

The Marketing Domain builds upon:

- Domain

Additional Marketing specifications define the detailed behavior of individual Marketing Objects.

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- marketing automation platforms;
- advertising systems;
- CRM software;
- campaign management tools;
- implementation technologies.

The Marketing Domain remains implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Marketing implementation shall:

- define owned Marketing Objects;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- support governed Lifecycles;
- expose standardized Capabilities;
- maintain interoperability with other OCOM Domains.
