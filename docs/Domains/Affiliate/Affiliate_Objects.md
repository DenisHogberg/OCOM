# Affiliate Objects

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-AFFILIATE-OBJECTS-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Entities owned by the Affiliate Domain.

Affiliate Objects represent enterprise affiliate partnerships, referral programs, commercial collaboration, partner performance, and attribution while preserving Object Ownership and enterprise interoperability.

Objects remain stable business concepts independent of implementation technologies.

---

# Definition

An Affiliate Object is a business entity owned by the Affiliate Domain.

Affiliate Objects describe affiliate partners, referral mechanisms, commercial agreements, tracking assets, and performance records.

Object semantics are defined by the Core Object specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Affiliate Objects provide a consistent representation of enterprise partner relationships.

They enable organizations to establish, manage, evaluate, and optimize affiliate ecosystems while maintaining governance, traceability, and accountability.

---

# Design Principles

Affiliate Objects shall:

- possess stable business identity;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- participate in governed Lifecycles;
- support Relationships with other Objects;
- produce Events throughout their Lifecycles;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Object Categories

Affiliate Objects may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Partner Objects

Objects representing affiliate business partners.

Examples include:

- Affiliate;
- Partner;
- Affiliate Organization;
- Affiliate Account;
- Partner Portfolio.

---

## Referral Objects

Objects representing referral mechanisms.

Examples include:

- Referral Program;
- Referral Campaign;
- Referral Attribution;
- Referral Rule;
- Referral Source.

Referral Objects define how partner-generated business opportunities are identified and attributed.

---

## Tracking Objects

Objects representing attribution assets.

Examples include:

- Tracking Link;
- Tracking Identifier;
- Tracking Token;
- Referral Code;
- Attribution Record.

Tracking Objects establish business attribution independently of implementation technologies.

---

## Commercial Objects

Objects representing commercial collaboration.

Examples include:

- Commission Plan;
- Partner Tier;
- Incentive Program;
- Partnership Agreement Reference;
- Revenue Share Profile.

Commercial terms may reference Contracts owned by other Domains without transferring Ownership.

---

## Performance Objects

Objects representing affiliate performance.

Examples include:

- Performance Profile;
- Partner Scorecard;
- Traffic Profile;
- Quality Assessment;
- Performance Summary.

Performance Objects describe business evaluation rather than analytical reports.

---

## Operational Objects

Objects supporting affiliate operations.

Examples include:

- Partner Assignment;
- Affiliate Manager Assignment;
- Review Schedule;
- Compliance Review Reference;
- Communication Session.

---

# Object Ownership

The Affiliate Domain owns Affiliate Objects.

Ownership includes:

- identity;
- lifecycle;
- governance;
- business semantics.

Other Domains may reference Affiliate Objects without assuming Ownership.

---

# Cross-Domain Relationships

Affiliate Objects commonly relate to:

- Customers;
- Products;
- Marketing Campaigns;
- Payments;
- Financial Records;
- Compliance Assessments;
- KPIs.

Relationships do not transfer Ownership.

---

# Object Governance

Affiliate Objects shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- Lifecycles;
- Policies;
- Relationships;
- Events.

Governance shall preserve consistency, traceability, and interoperability.

---

# Object Evolution

Affiliate Objects may evolve through:

- additional attributes;
- refined classifications;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements;
- expanded Relationships.

Object identity shall remain stable throughout evolution.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Affiliate Objects build upon:

- Object
- Relationship
- Lifecycle
- Event
- Policy
- Domain Governance

Additional Affiliate specifications define the behavior of individual Object categories.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- affiliate software;
- tracking platforms;
- attribution engines;
- partner portals;
- implementation technologies.

Affiliate Objects remain implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Affiliate implementation shall:

- define owned Affiliate Objects;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- assign governed Lifecycles;
- support standardized Relationships;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
