# Affiliate Processes

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-AFFILIATE-PROCESSES-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the business Processes coordinated by the Affiliate Domain.

Affiliate Processes coordinate the evolution of Affiliate Objects while preserving Object Ownership, Lifecycle integrity, enterprise governance, and interoperability.

Processes orchestrate business activities but do not own Entities.

---

# Definition

An Affiliate Process is a coordinated sequence of business activities performed to achieve an affiliate-related business outcome.

Processes operate on Affiliate Objects according to applicable Policies and Lifecycles.

Process semantics are defined by the Core Process specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Affiliate Processes enable organizations to consistently recruit, onboard, manage, evaluate, compensate, and optimize affiliate partnerships.

They provide structured operational workflows while preserving the authoritative state of Affiliate Objects.

---

# Design Principles

Affiliate Processes shall:

- coordinate Entities;
- preserve Object Ownership;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- produce Affiliate Events;
- support enterprise interoperability;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Process Categories

Affiliate Processes may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Partner Onboarding

Processes governing the establishment of affiliate partnerships.

Typical activities include:

- partner registration;
- eligibility verification;
- due diligence;
- approval;
- account creation;
- partner activation.

---

## Partner Management

Processes governing ongoing partner relationships.

Typical activities include:

- partner communication;
- account maintenance;
- performance review;
- relationship management;
- partner suspension;
- partner reinstatement.

---

## Referral Program Management

Processes governing Referral Programs.

Typical activities include:

- program creation;
- eligibility definition;
- publication;
- modification;
- suspension;
- retirement.

---

## Attribution Management

Processes governing referral attribution.

Typical activities include:

- tracking assignment;
- referral validation;
- attribution verification;
- attribution correction;
- attribution reconciliation.

Attribution Processes coordinate business attribution independently of implementation technologies.

---

## Commission Management

Processes governing affiliate compensation.

Typical activities include:

- commission calculation;
- commission approval;
- commission adjustment;
- incentive management;
- payout preparation.

Payment execution remains outside the Affiliate Domain.

---

## Performance Management

Processes governing affiliate performance.

Typical activities include:

- performance evaluation;
- quality assessment;
- traffic review;
- partner ranking;
- tier management.

---

## Compliance Management

Processes supporting affiliate governance.

Typical activities include:

- compliance review;
- policy validation;
- partner verification;
- operational audit;
- corrective action coordination.

Compliance decisions remain owned by the Compliance Domain.

---

## Partnership Improvement

Processes supporting continuous improvement.

Typical activities include:

- opportunity identification;
- partnership optimization;
- program refinement;
- partner development;
- strategic portfolio review.

---

# Process Ownership

The Affiliate Domain owns Affiliate Processes.

Processes coordinate Affiliate Objects but do not assume ownership of those Objects.

Ownership remains with the originating Domain.

---

# Process Governance

Affiliate Processes shall be governed through:

- Policies;
- Constraints;
- Lifecycle compatibility;
- approvals;
- operational standards.

Governance shall ensure predictable and auditable affiliate operations.

---

# Process Evolution

Affiliate Processes may evolve through:

- additional activities;
- improved coordination;
- refined governance;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Process evolution shall preserve Affiliate Object semantics.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Affiliate Processes build upon:

- Process
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Event
- Policy
- Capability
- Domain Governance

Additional Affiliate specifications define which Objects and Events participate in individual Processes.

---

# Independence

This specification does not prescribe:

- affiliate platforms;
- partner portals;
- workflow engines;
- attribution software;
- implementation technologies.

Affiliate Processes remain implementation independent.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Affiliate implementation shall:

- define standardized Affiliate Processes;
- coordinate Affiliate Objects;
- preserve Lifecycle integrity;
- emit appropriate Events;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
