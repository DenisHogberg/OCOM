# Affiliate Key Performance Indicators

**Document ID:** DOMAIN-AFFILIATE-KPI-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) applicable to the Affiliate Domain.

Affiliate KPIs provide standardized business measures for evaluating the effectiveness, efficiency, quality, governance, and maturity of enterprise affiliate operations.

KPIs enable organizations to continuously improve partner management while preserving Domain Ownership and enterprise interoperability.

---

# Definition

An Affiliate KPI is a measurable business indicator used to evaluate the performance of Affiliate Objects, Affiliate Processes, and Affiliate Capabilities.

Affiliate KPIs measure business outcomes owned by the Affiliate Domain rather than customer relationships, financial accounting, or technical tracking infrastructure.

KPI semantics are defined by the Core KPI specification.

---

# Business Meaning

Affiliate KPIs provide objective insight into how effectively the enterprise recruits, manages, develops, and governs affiliate partnerships.

KPIs support operational excellence, strategic decision-making, governance, and continuous improvement of the affiliate ecosystem.

---

# Design Principles

Affiliate KPIs shall:

- measure business outcomes;
- align with Affiliate Capabilities;
- remain objectively measurable;
- preserve semantic consistency;
- support historical comparison;
- remain technology independent.

---

# KPI Categories

Affiliate KPIs may be organized into the following categories.

---

## Partner Performance

Measures evaluating affiliate partnerships.

Examples include:

- active Partners;
- Partner activation rate;
- Partner retention rate;
- Partner lifecycle duration;
- Partner portfolio growth.

---

## Referral Performance

Measures evaluating Referral Programs.

Examples include:

- active Referral Programs;
- Referral Program participation;
- Referral attribution accuracy;
- referral conversion rate;
- Referral Program completion rate.

---

## Attribution Performance

Measures evaluating attribution quality.

Examples include:

- attribution completeness;
- attribution validation rate;
- attribution reconciliation rate;
- unresolved attribution cases;
- attribution consistency.

---

## Commission Performance

Measures evaluating commercial collaboration.

Examples include:

- Commission Plan utilization;
- commission approval cycle time;
- incentive participation;
- commission adjustment frequency;
- Partner tier distribution.

Commission payment execution is measured by the Payments Domain.

---

## Partner Quality

Measures evaluating partner quality.

Examples include:

- Partner quality score;
- traffic quality index;
- compliance score;
- partner review completion rate;
- partner maturity index.

---

## Operational Performance

Measures evaluating Affiliate operations.

Examples include:

- onboarding cycle time;
- Partner response time;
- portfolio coverage;
- operational efficiency;
- Affiliate Manager workload.

---

## Governance Performance

Measures evaluating Affiliate governance.

Examples include:

- Policy compliance rate;
- governance review completion;
- operational audit completion;
- governance exception rate;
- documentation completeness.

---

## Partnership Improvement

Measures evaluating continuous improvement.

Examples include:

- Partner development rate;
- portfolio optimization rate;
- strategic partnership growth;
- improvement initiative completion;
- partner satisfaction.

---

# KPI Ownership

Every KPI shall have an owning Domain.

The Affiliate Domain owns KPIs evaluating Affiliate Objects, Affiliate Processes, and Affiliate Capabilities.

Ownership of a KPI does not imply ownership of referenced Objects.

---

# KPI Calculation

Organizations shall define calculation methods appropriate to their affiliate environment.

Calculation methods may include:

- counts;
- percentages;
- ratios;
- duration measurements;
- trend analysis;
- maturity scoring.

Calculation formulas are implementation-specific.

---

# KPI Interpretation

KPIs shall be interpreted within their business context.

Interpretation may consider:

- partnership strategy;
- business objectives;
- partner portfolio characteristics;
- historical performance;
- applicable Policies.

Performance targets remain organization-specific.

---

# KPI Governance

Affiliate KPIs shall be governed through:

- Ownership;
- business definitions;
- calculation rules;
- version management;
- periodic review.

Governance ensures enterprise-wide consistency.

---

# KPI Evolution

Affiliate KPIs may evolve through:

- additional indicators;
- refined business definitions;
- improved calculation methods;
- compatibility-preserving enhancements.

Breaking semantic changes shall require explicit governance.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Affiliate KPIs build upon:

- KPI
- Capability
- Object
- Lifecycle
- Domain
- Policy
- Governance

Additional Affiliate specifications define the business context in which KPIs are evaluated.

---

# Independence

The Affiliate KPI specification does not prescribe:

- analytics platforms;
- affiliate dashboards;
- tracking software;
- reporting systems;
- implementation technologies.

KPIs remain logical business measures independent of implementation.

---

# Conformance

A conforming Affiliate implementation shall:

- define measurable Affiliate KPIs;
- assign KPI Ownership;
- provide consistent KPI definitions;
- govern KPI evolution;
- remain compatible with the OCOM Specification.
