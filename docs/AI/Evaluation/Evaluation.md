# Evaluation

**Document ID:** AI-Evaluation-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 20 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines Evaluation as the governed assessment of AI operations within the OCOM Artificial Intelligence Specification.

Evaluation measures the effectiveness, quality, reliability, and governance of AI-enabled operations to support continuous organizational improvement.

---

# Definition

Evaluation is the systematic assessment of AI operations against defined objectives, organizational policies, and operational expectations.

Evaluation may apply to individual components, complete executions, or overall business outcomes.

Evaluation is independent of specific AI models or implementation technologies.

---

# Business Meaning

Evaluation enables organizations to determine whether AI operations achieved their intended objectives while complying with governance requirements.

Evaluation provides evidence for operational improvement, risk reduction, and organizational learning.

---

# Core Principles

Every Evaluation shall be:

- objective-driven;
- measurable;
- explainable;
- reproducible;
- auditable;
- governed;
- technology independent.

---

# Mandatory Attributes

Every Evaluation shall define:

- Identifier
- Evaluation Target
- Objective
- Evaluation Time
- Evaluator
- Result

---

# Optional Attributes

An Evaluation may define:

- Description
- Evaluation Criteria
- Business Outcome
- Supporting Evidence
- Recommendations
- Confidence
- Related Workflow
- Related Agent

---

# Evaluation Targets

Evaluation may assess:

- AI Agents;
- Context;
- Memory;
- Knowledge;
- Tools;
- Prompts;
- Workflows;
- Entities;
- Complete AI Operations.

Organizations may define additional evaluation targets.

---

# Evaluation Sources

Evaluation may use information from:

- execution history;
- operational metrics;
- human review;
- AI review;
- audit records;
- business outcomes;
- customer feedback.

Organizations may combine multiple sources.

---

# Evaluation Outcomes

Typical outcomes include:

- Successful;
- Acceptable;
- Needs Improvement;
- Failed;
- Non-Compliant.

Organizations may define additional outcomes.

---

# Relationships

Evaluation may reference:

- AI Agents;
- Context;
- Memory;
- Knowledge;
- Tools;
- Prompts;
- Events;
- Workflows.

Relationships shall preserve traceability.

---

# Governance

Evaluation shall comply with:

- organizational policies;
- review procedures;
- quality standards;
- audit requirements.

Evaluation shall preserve transparency and accountability.

---

# Explainability

Organizations shall be able to determine:

- what was evaluated;
- why it was evaluated;
- which criteria were applied;
- what evidence supported the result;
- what recommendations were produced.

---

# Auditability

Every Evaluation should preserve:

- Evaluation Identifier;
- evaluator;
- timestamp;
- evaluation criteria;
- outcome;
- supporting evidence.

Audit records shall remain immutable.

---

# Independence

The Evaluation specification does not prescribe:

- scoring systems;
- benchmark datasets;
- AI providers;
- statistical methods;
- implementation technologies.

Organizations remain free to implement Evaluation using any compatible methodology.

---

# Conformance

A compliant implementation shall:

- define Evaluation targets;
- preserve evaluation history;
- support explainability;
- maintain auditability;
- remain technology independent.
