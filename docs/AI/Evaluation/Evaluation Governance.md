# Evaluation Governance

**Document ID:** AI-Evaluation-04

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 20 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the governance framework for Evaluation within the OCOM Artificial Intelligence Specification.

Evaluation Governance establishes the policies, responsibilities, controls, and approval mechanisms required to ensure that evaluations remain objective, trustworthy, and suitable for organizational decision-making.

---

# Definition

Evaluation Governance is the organizational framework that governs how Evaluations are planned, performed, reviewed, approved, and maintained.

Governance ensures that Evaluation results are reliable, explainable, reproducible, and aligned with organizational objectives.

---

# Design Principles

Evaluation Governance shall:

- establish accountability;
- preserve objectivity;
- ensure transparency;
- maintain auditability;
- support continuous improvement;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Governance Objectives

Evaluation Governance aims to:

- ensure evaluation consistency;
- support trusted decision-making;
- prevent biased evaluations;
- maintain evaluation quality;
- support regulatory compliance;
- preserve organizational confidence.

---

# Governance Roles

Organizations should define governance roles including:

- Evaluation Owner;
- Evaluator;
- Reviewer;
- Approver;
- Auditor;
- Business Sponsor.

Organizations may define additional governance roles.

---

# Ownership

Every Evaluation shall have an assigned owner responsible for:

- evaluation planning;
- execution oversight;
- evidence completeness;
- review coordination;
- result publication.

Ownership shall remain traceable.

---

# Independence

Evaluators should remain sufficiently independent from the evaluated activity whenever organizational policy requires objective assessment.

Organizations shall define independence requirements appropriate to business risk.

---

# Approval

Organizations shall define approval procedures appropriate to evaluation scope and business impact.

Approval may include:

- single approver;
- multiple approvers;
- committee review;
- automated governance validation.

Approval history shall be preserved.

---

# Evidence Management

Evaluation Governance shall ensure that:

- evidence is preserved;
- evidence remains traceable;
- evidence integrity is maintained;
- evidence sources are identifiable.

Evaluation conclusions should be supported by appropriate evidence.

---

# Conflict Management

Organizations should define procedures for managing:

- conflicting evidence;
- conflicting evaluations;
- disputed conclusions;
- inconsistent recommendations.

Conflict resolution shall preserve audit history.

---

# Access Control

Evaluation information shall be protected according to:

- organizational roles;
- security classifications;
- privacy requirements;
- regulatory obligations.

Access policies are organization-specific.

---

# Auditability

Organizations shall preserve:

- ownership history;
- review history;
- approval history;
- governance decisions;
- evidence references;
- policy revisions.

Audit records shall remain immutable.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Evaluation Governance interacts with:

- Evaluation
- Evaluation Criteria
- Evaluation Lifecycle
- Continuous Improvement
- Evidence Overlay
- Confidence

---

# Independence

The Evaluation Governance specification does not prescribe:

- governance platforms;
- workflow systems;
- approval technologies;
- implementation architectures.

Organizations remain free to implement governance using any compatible approach.

---

# Conformance

A compliant implementation shall:

- define governance responsibilities;
- assign ownership;
- preserve evaluation evidence;
- support objective review;
- maintain auditability.
