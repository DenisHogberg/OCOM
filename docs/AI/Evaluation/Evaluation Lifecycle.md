# Evaluation Lifecycle

**Document ID:** AI-Evaluation-03

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 20 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the lifecycle of Evaluation within the OCOM Artificial Intelligence Specification.

The Evaluation Lifecycle establishes the governed states through which an Evaluation progresses from initiation to completion and archival.

---

# Definition

The Evaluation Lifecycle represents the controlled progression of an Evaluation throughout its operational existence.

The lifecycle ensures that evaluations are planned, executed, reviewed, and retained in a consistent and auditable manner.

---

# Design Principles

The Evaluation Lifecycle shall:

- preserve objectivity;
- support reproducibility;
- maintain auditability;
- preserve traceability;
- support continuous improvement;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Lifecycle States

The OCOM Specification defines the following lifecycle states.

## Requested

An Evaluation has been requested.

Characteristics:

- evaluation target identified;
- evaluation objective defined;
- evaluation not yet started.

---

## Planned

The Evaluation approach has been prepared.

Typical activities include:

- selecting evaluation criteria;
- identifying evidence sources;
- assigning evaluator;
- defining evaluation scope.

---

## In Progress

Evaluation activities are being performed.

Typical activities include:

- collecting evidence;
- analyzing results;
- validating findings;
- documenting observations.

---

## Completed

Evaluation has concluded.

Characteristics:

- outcome determined;
- evidence recorded;
- recommendations prepared.

---

## Reviewed

Evaluation results have undergone review.

Review may include:

- expert validation;
- governance review;
- quality assurance;
- compliance verification.

---

## Approved

Evaluation results have been formally accepted.

Approved evaluations become official organizational records.

---

## Archived

Evaluation is no longer active.

Historical records remain available for:

- audit;
- compliance;
- operational analysis;
- continuous improvement.

---

# Lifecycle Transitions

Typical lifecycle progression:

Requested

↓

Planned

↓

In Progress

↓

Completed

↓

Reviewed

↓

Approved

↓

Archived

Organizations may define additional lifecycle states.

---

# Ownership

Every Evaluation shall have an assigned evaluator.

Organizations may additionally assign:

- reviewer;
- approver;
- audit owner.

Ownership shall remain traceable.

---

# Governance

Lifecycle transitions shall comply with:

- organizational policies;
- approval procedures;
- review requirements;
- audit policies.

---

# Auditability

Organizations shall preserve:

- lifecycle history;
- evaluator history;
- review history;
- approval history;
- archival information.

Audit records shall remain immutable.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

The Evaluation Lifecycle interacts with:

- Evaluation
- Evaluation Criteria
- Evaluation Governance
- Continuous Improvement
- Evidence Overlay

---

# Independence

The Evaluation Lifecycle specification does not prescribe:

- workflow engines;
- approval platforms;
- implementation technologies.

Organizations remain free to implement lifecycle management using any compatible architecture.

---

# Conformance

A compliant implementation shall:

- support governed lifecycle states;
- preserve lifecycle history;
- maintain auditability;
- support controlled evaluation processes.
