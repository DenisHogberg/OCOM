# Evaluation Criteria

**Document ID:** AI-Evaluation-02

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 20 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the evaluation criteria used to assess AI operations within the OCOM Artificial Intelligence Specification.

Evaluation Criteria provide a common framework for measuring operational quality, governance, reliability, and business effectiveness across AI components.

---

# Definition

Evaluation Criteria are measurable characteristics used to assess whether an Evaluation Target satisfies defined organizational objectives and operational expectations.

Evaluation Criteria shall be objective, explainable, and applicable to one or more Evaluation Targets.

---

# Design Principles

Evaluation Criteria shall:

- support business objectives;
- be measurable;
- be reproducible;
- preserve explainability;
- support governance;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Standard Evaluation Criteria

The OCOM Specification defines the following standard evaluation criteria.

## Objective Fulfillment

Measures whether the intended business objective was achieved.

---

## Accuracy

Measures the correctness of produced outputs.

---

## Completeness

Measures whether all required information or actions were provided.

---

## Consistency

Measures alignment with organizational Knowledge, policies, and previous decisions.

---

## Relevance

Measures whether information, reasoning, or actions remained aligned with the execution objective.

---

## Timeliness

Measures whether execution occurred within acceptable operational time.

---

## Reliability

Measures the stability and repeatability of execution outcomes.

---

## Explainability

Measures the ability to explain:

- reasoning;
- decisions;
- data sources;
- execution path.

---

## Traceability

Measures whether execution can be reconstructed from available records.

---

## Governance Compliance

Measures adherence to:

- organizational policies;
- approval requirements;
- security controls;
- regulatory obligations.

---

## Risk Management

Measures whether operational risks were appropriately identified and managed.

---

## Business Impact

Measures the operational value created by the execution.

Business Impact assessment is organization-specific.

---

# Target Applicability

Evaluation Criteria may be applied to:

- AI Agents;
- Context;
- Knowledge;
- Memory;
- Tools;
- Prompts;
- Workflows;
- Entities;
- Operational Executions.

Organizations may define additional Evaluation Targets.

---

# Organization-Specific Criteria

Organizations may define additional Evaluation Criteria appropriate to their operational environment.

Additional criteria shall preserve explainability and governance.

---

# Evidence

Every Evaluation Criterion should be supported by evidence where appropriate.

Evidence may include:

- execution history;
- audit records;
- operational metrics;
- business outcomes;
- expert review.

---

# Governance

Evaluation Criteria shall be:

- documented;
- versioned;
- approved;
- periodically reviewed.

Changes shall preserve traceability.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Evaluation Criteria interact with:

- Evaluation
- Evaluation Governance
- Continuous Improvement
- Evidence Overlay
- Confidence

---

# Independence

The Evaluation Criteria specification does not prescribe:

- scoring algorithms;
- mathematical models;
- benchmark datasets;
- implementation technologies.

Organizations remain free to implement evaluation methodologies using any compatible approach.

---

# Conformance

A compliant implementation shall:

- define measurable Evaluation Criteria;
- preserve evaluation consistency;
- support explainability;
- maintain governance;
- remain technology independent.
