# Knowledge Quality

**Document ID:** AI-Knowledge-05

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 20 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the quality principles and evaluation criteria for organizational Knowledge within the OCOM Artificial Intelligence Specification.

Knowledge Quality ensures that organizational Knowledge remains reliable, consistent, explainable, and suitable for operational use.

---

# Definition

Knowledge Quality is the degree to which organizational Knowledge satisfies business, operational, and governance requirements.

Knowledge Quality shall be continuously assessed throughout the Knowledge Lifecycle.

---

# Design Principles

Knowledge Quality shall:

- support business objectives;
- preserve consistency;
- maintain explainability;
- support governance;
- enable continuous improvement;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Quality Objectives

Knowledge Quality aims to:

- improve decision-making;
- increase AI reliability;
- reduce ambiguity;
- minimize inconsistencies;
- support regulatory compliance;
- preserve organizational trust.

---

# Quality Dimensions

Knowledge should be evaluated according to:

- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Consistency
- Relevance
- Timeliness
- Traceability
- Explainability
- Version Integrity

Organizations may define additional quality dimensions.

---

# Quality Assessment

Knowledge may be assessed through:

- expert review;
- policy validation;
- consistency checks;
- operational feedback;
- audit activities.

Assessment methods are organization-specific.

---

# Quality Indicators

Typical indicators include:

- review frequency;
- approval status;
- version currency;
- conflict count;
- evidence availability;
- provenance completeness.

Organizations may define additional indicators.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Knowledge Quality should improve through:

- periodic reviews;
- operational feedback;
- incident analysis;
- policy updates;
- expert recommendations;
- governance improvements.

---

# Quality Issues

Organizations should identify and manage issues including:

- outdated Knowledge;
- conflicting Knowledge;
- incomplete Knowledge;
- duplicate Knowledge;
- unsupported Knowledge;
- unapproved Knowledge.

Quality issues shall be resolved through governed processes.

---

# Governance

Knowledge Quality shall comply with:

- organizational policies;
- approval procedures;
- review schedules;
- audit requirements.

Quality evaluations shall preserve traceability.

---

# Auditability

Organizations shall preserve:

- quality assessments;
- review history;
- identified issues;
- corrective actions;
- approval records.

Audit information shall remain immutable.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Knowledge Quality interacts with:

- Knowledge
- Knowledge Sources
- Knowledge Lifecycle
- Knowledge Governance
- Evidence Overlay
- Confidence

---

# Independence

The Knowledge Quality specification does not prescribe:

- scoring models;
- quality metrics;
- review tools;
- governance platforms.

Organizations remain free to implement quality management using any compatible methodology.

---

# Conformance

A compliant implementation shall:

- define Knowledge quality criteria;
- support periodic assessment;
- preserve assessment history;
- support continuous improvement;
- maintain technology independence.
