# Knowledge

**Document ID:** AI-Knowledge-00

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 20 July 2026

---

# Purpose

The Knowledge Specification defines how organizations represent, organize, govern, and consume business knowledge within the OCOM Artificial Intelligence Specification.

Knowledge provides stable organizational understanding that supports reasoning, planning, and decision-making across AI Agents and business processes.

---

# Definition

Knowledge is structured organizational information that represents business facts, concepts, policies, relationships, and operational understanding.

Unlike operational Context, Knowledge is persistent and reusable across multiple executions.

Knowledge is independent of individual AI Agents and implementation technologies.

---

# Core Principles

Knowledge shall be:

- persistent;
- governed;
- reusable;
- explainable;
- traceable;
- versioned;
- technology independent.

---

# Objectives

The Knowledge Specification enables organizations to:

- capture business knowledge;
- standardize organizational understanding;
- improve AI reasoning;
- support consistent decision-making;
- reduce duplicated knowledge;
- preserve institutional expertise.

---

# Architecture

The Knowledge Specification consists of the following documents:

- Knowledge
- Knowledge Sources
- Knowledge Lifecycle
- Knowledge Governance
- Knowledge Quality

Each document defines one aspect of organizational knowledge management.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Knowledge interacts with:

- Agents
- Context
- Memory
- Entities
- Events
- Workflows
- Business Rules

Knowledge provides stable organizational understanding that may be used during Context Assembly and AI reasoning.

---

# Technology Independence

The Knowledge Specification does not prescribe:

- knowledge graphs;
- vector databases;
- graph databases;
- document stores;
- ontologies;
- semantic technologies.

Organizations may implement Knowledge using any compatible technology.

---

# Conformance

A compliant implementation shall:

- define governed organizational knowledge;
- support reuse across executions;
- preserve provenance;
- maintain explainability;
- remain technology independent.
