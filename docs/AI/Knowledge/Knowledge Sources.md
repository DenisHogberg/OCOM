# Knowledge Sources

**Document ID:** AI-Knowledge-02

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 20 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the sources from which organizational Knowledge may be created, maintained, and updated within the OCOM Artificial Intelligence Specification.

Knowledge Sources establish the origin of organizational understanding while preserving provenance and governance.

---

# Definition

A Knowledge Source is any governed origin of information that contributes to organizational Knowledge.

Knowledge may be derived from one or multiple sources.

Every Knowledge Source shall preserve traceability.

---

# Design Principles

Knowledge Sources shall:

- be identifiable;
- preserve provenance;
- support governance;
- remain explainable;
- support versioning;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Source Categories

Knowledge may originate from:

- Organizational Documentation;
- Business Policies;
- Operational Procedures;
- Business Rules;
- Contracts;
- Regulatory Requirements;
- Industry Standards;
- Domain Experts;
- Historical Operational Experience;
- Approved External Sources.

Organizations may define additional source categories.

---

# Human Sources

Knowledge may be created or validated by:

- subject matter experts;
- business owners;
- department managers;
- compliance specialists;
- executive leadership.

Human contributions shall preserve authorship.

---

# System Sources

Knowledge may originate from:

- enterprise systems;
- workflow platforms;
- document repositories;
- operational databases;
- knowledge management systems.

System-generated Knowledge shall preserve system provenance.

---

# External Sources

Knowledge may incorporate approved external information, including:

- regulatory publications;
- industry standards;
- partner documentation;
- vendor documentation;
- publicly available reference materials.

Organizations shall define approval policies for external sources.

---

# Source Validation

Organizations shall define validation procedures before incorporating Knowledge.

Validation may include:

- expert review;
- policy approval;
- compliance verification;
- consistency checks;
- quality assessment.

---

# Provenance

Every Knowledge Source shall preserve:

- source identifier;
- source type;
- author or originating system;
- acquisition time;
- approval status;
- applicable permissions.

---

# Governance

Knowledge Sources shall comply with:

- ownership policies;
- access permissions;
- approval workflows;
- security classifications;
- retention requirements.

Unapproved sources shall not become governed Knowledge.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Knowledge Sources interact with:

- Knowledge
- Memory
- Context
- Business Rules
- Entities
- Events

Knowledge Sources provide the foundation from which governed Knowledge is established.

---

# Independence

The Knowledge Sources specification does not prescribe:

- document management systems;
- enterprise repositories;
- knowledge graph technologies;
- storage platforms;
- ingestion mechanisms.

Organizations remain free to implement any compatible architecture.

---

# Conformance

A compliant implementation shall:

- identify Knowledge Sources;
- preserve provenance;
- support governance;
- maintain explainability;
- validate organizational Knowledge before adoption.
