# Knowledge Governance

**Document ID:** AI-Knowledge-04

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 20 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the governance framework for organizational Knowledge within the OCOM Artificial Intelligence Specification.

Knowledge Governance establishes accountability, ownership, quality control, and decision authority throughout the Knowledge Lifecycle.

---

# Definition

Knowledge Governance is the set of organizational policies, roles, and controls that ensure Knowledge remains accurate, trustworthy, explainable, and aligned with business objectives.

Governance applies throughout the entire lifecycle of Knowledge.

---

# Design Principles

Knowledge Governance shall:

- establish clear ownership;
- ensure accountability;
- preserve provenance;
- support version control;
- maintain explainability;
- support auditability;
- remain technology independent.

---

# Governance Objectives

Knowledge Governance aims to:

- maintain Knowledge quality;
- prevent unauthorized changes;
- ensure organizational consistency;
- support regulatory compliance;
- preserve institutional knowledge;
- reduce operational risk.

---

# Governance Roles

Organizations should define governance roles including:

- Knowledge Owner;
- Knowledge Contributor;
- Reviewer;
- Approver;
- Consumer;
- Auditor.

Organizations may define additional governance roles.

---

# Ownership

Every Knowledge object shall have an assigned owner.

The owner is responsible for:

- accuracy;
- maintenance;
- review scheduling;
- approval coordination;
- retirement decisions.

Ownership shall be traceable.

---

# Change Management

Knowledge modifications shall follow governed procedures.

Typical change activities include:

- proposing changes;
- reviewing updates;
- validating accuracy;
- approving revisions;
- publishing new versions.

Unauthorized modifications shall not become Active Knowledge.

---

# Approval

Organizations shall define approval procedures appropriate to business risk.

Approval may involve:

- single approver;
- multiple approvers;
- committee review;
- automated policy validation.

Approval history shall be preserved.

---

# Access Control

Knowledge access shall comply with:

- organizational roles;
- permissions;
- security classifications;
- regulatory requirements.

Access policies shall apply independently of implementation technology.

---

# Compliance

Knowledge Governance shall support compliance with:

- organizational policies;
- contractual obligations;
- regulatory requirements;
- internal governance standards.

Compliance requirements may vary by business domain.

---

# Auditability

Organizations shall preserve:

- ownership history;
- approval history;
- modification history;
- version history;
- access history.

Audit records shall remain immutable.

---

# Relationship to Other Specifications

Knowledge Governance interacts with:

- Knowledge
- Knowledge Lifecycle
- Knowledge Sources
- Memory
- Context
- Business Rules

---

# Independence

The Knowledge Governance specification does not prescribe:

- governance platforms;
- workflow systems;
- document repositories;
- approval technologies.

Organizations remain free to implement governance using any compatible architecture.

---

# Conformance

A compliant implementation shall:

- define governance responsibilities;
- assign ownership;
- preserve approval history;
- support controlled modifications;
- maintain auditability.
