# AI Architecture

**Document ID:** REF-AI-01

**Status:** Informative

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the AI Architecture of the OCOM Reference Architecture.

It describes the architectural role of Artificial Intelligence within an object-centric enterprise while preserving business authority, governance, and operational integrity.

This document is informative.

---

# Architectural Perspective

Artificial Intelligence is an architectural capability that augments enterprise operations.

AI analyzes Entities, Business Events, Operational Memory, and enterprise context to support business decision-making.

AI does not replace Entities, Domains, or Governance.

---

# Role of Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence assists enterprise operations by:

- analyzing business context;
- recognizing patterns;
- generating recommendations;
- summarizing operational knowledge;
- estimating outcomes;
- detecting anomalies.

AI supports enterprise decisions without becoming the source of business authority.

---

# AI Information Sources

Artificial Intelligence may utilize information from:

- Entities;
- Business Events;
- Operational Memory;
- Policies;
- Capabilities;
- enterprise metadata.

AI shall distinguish authoritative business information from contextual operational knowledge.

---

# AI Participation

Artificial Intelligence may participate in:

- operational analysis;
- planning;
- decision support;
- workflow assistance;
- knowledge discovery;
- enterprise optimization.

Participation does not imply ownership.

---

# AI Recommendations

AI recommendations are advisory.

Recommendations may:

- suggest actions;
- identify risks;
- prioritize work;
- recommend investigations;
- estimate confidence.

Recommendations remain subject to organizational governance.

---

# AI Decision Authority

Business authority always remains with the owning Domain.

Artificial Intelligence shall not become the authoritative source of:

- business state;
- policy interpretation;
- ownership decisions;
- regulatory compliance.

Organizations may automate decision execution through governed business processes.

---

# AI and Operational Memory

Artificial Intelligence both consumes and contributes Operational Memory.

AI may:

- generate observations;
- summarize historical context;
- identify missing information;
- estimate confidence.

AI-generated memory shall preserve:

- provenance;
- authorship;
- confidence;
- timestamps.

---

# AI Governance

Artificial Intelligence operates under enterprise governance.

Governance includes:

- authorization;
- explainability;
- traceability;
- accountability;
- auditability.

AI participation shall remain observable throughout enterprise operations.

---

# Architectural Characteristics

The AI Architecture exhibits the following characteristics:

- advisory;
- object-centric;
- memory-aware;
- governance-controlled;
- explainable;
- technology-independent.

---

# Relationship to Other Architectural Views

This architectural view complements:

- Enterprise Architecture
- Domain Architecture
- Business Object Architecture
- Business Event Architecture
- Operational Memory Architecture
- Governance Architecture
- Integration Architecture

Together these views define the complete OCOM Reference Architecture.
