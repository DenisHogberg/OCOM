# Examples

**Document ID:** EXAMPLES-README-01

**Status:** Draft

**Version:** 0.1

**Last Updated:** 21 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This section provides informative examples demonstrating how the OCOM Specification may be applied across different industries and business scenarios.

Examples are intended to illustrate architectural concepts, Entities, Lifecycles, Relationships, Capabilities, Policies, Memory, and AI within realistic enterprise environments.

Examples are non-normative and do not define additional requirements beyond the OCOM Specification.

---

# Scope

The Examples section demonstrates practical applications of OCOM across multiple industries.

Examples may include:

- Entities;
- Domain interactions;
- Cross-Domain workflows;
- Lifecycle transitions;
- AI-assisted operations;
- Memory integration;
- Governance scenarios;
- End-to-end business processes.

---

# Organization

Examples are organized by industry.

Current example collections include:

- FinTech
- Healthcare
- iGaming
- Manufacturing
- Marketplace
- SaaS

Additional industries may be introduced in future revisions.

---

# Objectives

Examples are intended to:

- demonstrate practical application of OCOM;
- illustrate Entity modeling;
- explain Cross-Domain collaboration;
- show enterprise AI integration;
- demonstrate Memory usage;
- provide reference implementations for architects.

---

# Consistency

All examples shall remain consistent with the normative OCOM Specification.

Examples shall not redefine:

- Entities;
- Domain Ownership;
- Lifecycle semantics;
- Policies;
- Relationships;
- Governance rules.

If an example conflicts with the normative specification, the specification takes precedence.

---

# Industry Independence

Industry examples demonstrate specialization without changing the core architecture.

The same OCOM principles apply regardless of industry.

---

# Example Structure

Individual examples should include, where applicable:

- Business Context
- Entities
- Participating Domains
- Relationships
- Lifecycle
- Events
- Capabilities
- Policies
- Memory
- AI Participation
- Expected Outcomes

Not every example is required to include every section.

---

# Conformance

Examples are informative.

Conformance is determined exclusively by the normative OCOM Specification.
